# Hypertrophy

## Definition
Hypertrophy is the increase in muscle fiber size (cross-sectional area) in response to resistance training.

## Scientific Basis
Muscle hypertrophy occurs through a complex process involving:
- Mechanical tension on muscle fibers
- Metabolic stress from metabolic byproducts
- Muscle damage from eccentric contractions
- Subsequent satellite cell activation and protein synthesis

## Evidence
The hypertrophy training literature is extensive. Key principles:
- Volume appears to be the primary driver of muscle growth [[Schoenfeld et al. 2017]]
- Intensity (load) matters less than proximity to failure [[Sanchez-Medina et al. 2019]]
- Frequency may enhance hypertrophy when volume is equated [[Schoenfeld et al. 2016]]

## Practical Applications
- Progressive overload of total training volume
- Rep ranges of 6-30 reps appear similarly effective
- Time under tension considerations
- Minimum effective volume thresholds (~10 sets/week per muscle group)
