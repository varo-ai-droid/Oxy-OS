# Muscle Fiber

## Definition
A muscle fiber (also called a muscle cell or myofiber) is a single contractile cell within a skeletal muscle. Each fiber contains myofibrils, which are the contractile elements composed of actin and myosin filaments.

## Fiber Types

### Type I (Slow Twitch)
- High oxidative capacity, low force production
- Fatigue-resistant, used for endurance activities
- Limited hypertrophy potential
- Recruited first according to Henneman's size principle

### Type II (Fast Twitch)
- **Type IIa**: Moderate oxidative capacity, high force production
- **Type IIx**: Low oxidative capacity, very high force production
- High hypertrophy potential
- Recruited only when high force output is needed

## Fiber-Specific Hypertrophy
- Hypertrophy is muscle-fiber specific — different fibers grow in response to different stimuli
- Heavy loads recruit high-threshold (Type II) fibers from the first repetition
- Light loads require metabolic fatigue accumulation to eventually recruit Type II fibers
- See [[Muscle Fiber Specificity]] for detailed coverage
