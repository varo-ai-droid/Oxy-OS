# Hypertrophy Stimulus Period

## Definition
The duration after a workout during which muscle fibers are in a state of net protein accretion (growth). After this period ends, atrophy begins as the muscle fibers return to baseline size.

## Scientific Basis
Based on analysis of muscle protein synthesis (MPS) and myofibrillar protein synthesis (MYOPS) data:

- The hypertrophy stimulus period is estimated to be approximately **36 hours** post-workout, not the commonly cited 48-72 hours
- Post-workout MPS/MYOPS elevations consist of both hypertrophy stimulus AND muscle damage repair processes occurring simultaneously
- Non-exercise muscle injury studies show MPS doesn't elevate until 24-36 hours after injury, supporting that damage repair occurs in the "fat tail" portion of the curve
- Single-set studies show MYOPS returning to baseline around 35-36 hours post-exercise

### Key Evidence Points
- Light loads (20-25 rep max) produce more MPS elevation than moderate loads (8-10 rep max) in the second 24-hour period due to increased muscle damage
- Long rest periods produce higher MPS in the FIRST 24 hours (hypertrophy stimulus) but not in the second 24 hours
- This indicates hypertrophy stimulus is largely concentrated in the initial 12-24 hours post-workout

## Implications for Training Frequency
- **Full body 3x per week**: With 36-hour stimulus period, creates ~5 days of protection, leaving only ~2 days of potential atrophy
- **Upper/Lower 4x per week**: Each muscle trained twice weekly may have ~3 days of atrophy between sessions
- Training every 36 hours would theoretically be optimal from a physiological standpoint, but practicality often dictates otherwise

## Contradictions/Questions
- Many sources still cite 48-72 hour stimulus periods without considering the damage repair component of MPS measurements
- The relationship between MPS duration and actual hypertrophy stimulus needs further clarification
