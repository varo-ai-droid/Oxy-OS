# One Rep Max

## Definition
The maximum amount of weight that can be lifted for a single repetition of a given exercise with proper form. 1RM is the gold standard measure of maximal strength.

## Relationship to Training
- 1RM is used to determine training loads as percentages (e.g., 70% 1RM)
- Heavy loads (70-90% 1RM) produce high mechanical tension and promote hypertrophy
- Loads above 90% 1RM are primarily strength-focused with minimal hypertrophy stimulus
- 1RM testing carries injury risk and is not always necessary for programming

## Practical Considerations
- Estimated 1RM can be calculated from submaximal loads (e.g., 5RM, 10RM)
- 1RM fluctuates based on fatigue state, recovery, and neural readiness
- Not all exercises need 1RM testing — machine exercises and isolation work can use RPE-based loading
