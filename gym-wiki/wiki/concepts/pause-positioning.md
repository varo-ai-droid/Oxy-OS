# Pause Positioning

## Definition
Strategic placement of pauses within exercise range of motion to target specific muscle regions or achieve specific physiological effects, based on matching muscle leverage with exercise resistance profiles.

## Scientific Basis
- **Leverage Matching**: Pauses should be placed where the target muscle has best leverage AND the exercise is most difficult
- **Fatigue Considerations**: 
  - Contract/mid-range pauses → metabolite-related fatigue (benign)
  - Stretch position pauses → calcium-ion-related fatigue (can interfere with subsequent reps)
- **Effort Levels**: Isometric contractions produce higher force per fiber than dynamic, but may have lower recruitment

## Key Principles
1. **Target muscle leverage determines pause position**:
   - Soleus has best leverage in contract position → pause at top for seated calf raises
   - Gastrocnemius has best leverage in stretch position → pause at bottom for calf raises

2. **Resistance profile determines effectiveness**:
   - Pauses are most effective when placed in the exercise's "sticking point" (hardest position)
   - If muscle leverage and resistance profile don't align, partial ROM is often superior to pauses

3. **Active vs Passive Stretch Distinction**:
   - Active stretch (muscle activated) = stretch position isometric
   - Passive stretch (muscle relaxed) = separate static stretching intervention
   - These produce different physiological effects and should not be conflated

## Practical Applications
- **Seated Calf Raise**: Pause at top (contract position) - muscle leverage and resistance profile align
- **Standing Calf Raise**: Partial ROM from stretch to mid-range better than pause at bottom
- **Leg Press/Squat**: Stretch position pause may provide stretch-mediated hypertrophy benefits
- **Avoid**: Passive stretching between reps (creates calcium-ion fatigue without benefit)

## Recommendations
- Use partial ROM instead of pauses when possible (easier to standardize)
- Match pause position to target muscle leverage AND exercise difficulty
- Do passive stretching after workout, not during
- Static stretches after training show synergistic effects with stretch-position partials
