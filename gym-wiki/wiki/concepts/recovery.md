# Recovery

## Definition
The process by which the body returns to baseline physiological state after training, including repair of muscle damage, clearance of fatigue mechanisms, and restoration of performance capacity.

## Volume-Recovery Relationship

### Recovery Timeline by Sets
A linear relationship exists between the number of sets per muscle per workout and recovery time:
- **1-2 sets to failure**: ~24-48 hours recovery
- **3 sets to failure**: ~48 hours recovery
- **5 sets to failure**: ~72 hours recovery
- **10-12 sets per muscle**: ~1 week recovery
- **Extreme volume**: Up to 4 weeks (rare, typically from eccentric-only protocols)

### Fatigue Debt Calculation
If volume exceeds recoverable levels, each excessive workout adds to a "fatigue debt":
- 3 workouts/week with 1 extra day of unrecovered fatigue = 3 days/week of accumulating debt
- After 4 weeks: ~12 days of accumulated fatigue needing recovery
- Maximum physiological ceiling: ~28 days (4 weeks) of accumulated debt

## Recovery vs Hypertrophy Stimulus

### They Are Separate Processes
- **Hypertrophy stimulus period** (~36 hours): Driven by MPS elevation, largely independent of muscle damage
- **Recovery/repair process** (variable): Driven by inflammatory response to muscle damage, separate from hypertrophic growth
- You can have the same amount of growth with completely different recovery processes, depending on how much muscle damage you create

### Factors Affecting Recovery
- **Volume**: Primary determinant of recovery time
- **Exercise selection**: Stretch-position exercises create more damage and require longer recovery
- **Eccentric emphasis**: Increases muscle damage and recovery time
- **Individual factors**: Age, training history, sleep quality, nutrition
- **Sleep restriction**: Increases MPB, decreases net protein balance
- **Caloric restriction**: Reduces MPS, increases recovery time

## Practical Applications

### Recoverable Volume Guidelines
- **Full body 3x/week**: Stay within 2-3 sets per muscle per session
- **Upper/Lower 4x/week**: Lower per-session volume to compensate for higher frequency
- **Bro split**: Higher per-session volume possible due to longer recovery windows

### Optimizing Recovery
- Keep per-session volume within recoverable limits
- Use different exercises across sessions to distribute fatigue across different fiber populations
- Ensure adequate sleep and nutrition
- Use deloads only when fatigue has accumulated significantly
