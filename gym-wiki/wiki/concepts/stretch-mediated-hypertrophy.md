# Stretch Mediated Hypertrophy

## Definition
Muscle growth triggered by passive tension from stretching, primarily resulting in sarcomerogenesis (addition of sarcomeres in series, lengthening muscle fibers) rather than true hypertrophy (increase in fiber cross-sectional area).

## Mechanism

### Titin-Driven Passive Tension
- Titin is the primary source of passive mechanical tension in muscle fibers
- The shape of the passive tension vs sarcomere length curve is identical whether measured at whole muscle, intact fiber, or skinned fiber level — proving titin drives the relationship
- When sarcomeres are stretched past a critical length threshold, titin triggers signaling pathways for sarcomere addition

### Sarcomerogenesis vs True Hypertrophy
- **Sarcomerogenesis**: Addition of sarcomeres in series — lengthens the muscle fiber, increases range of motion potential
- **True hypertrophy**: Addition of sarcomeres in parallel — increases fiber cross-sectional area, increases force production
- Stretch-mediated growth is primarily sarcomerogenesis, not true hypertrophy

### Fiber-Specific Effects
- Only occurs when individual sarcomeres are stretched past a critical length threshold
- Whole muscle lengthening alone doesn't trigger it — sarcomere lengthening is what matters
- Different muscle groups and fiber types have different stretch thresholds

## Practical Implications

### Stretch Position Training
- Exercises that place muscles in a lengthened position under load may stimulate sarcomerogenesis
- May be beneficial for increasing muscle length and range of motion
- Should be combined with traditional hypertrophy training for complete development

### Relationship to Muscle Damage
- Stretch-position exercises create more muscle damage than contracted-position exercises
- This increases recovery time and fatigue accumulation
- Balance stretch-mediated stimulus with recoverable volume
