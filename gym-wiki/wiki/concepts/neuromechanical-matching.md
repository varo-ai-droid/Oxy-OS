# Neuromechanical Matching

## Definition
The principle that the central nervous system activates muscle regions based on mechanical leverage efficiency to accomplish movements. Regions with better leverage are prioritized, creating voluntary activation deficits in other regions even at maximal effort.

## Scientific Basis
- CNS sends motor commands to regions with the best leverage first
- Within each region, Henneman's size principle applies (smaller motor units recruited first)
- When the optimal region reaches capacity, other regions begin to activate, but with less efficiency
- The brain has a limit on central motor command due to maximum tolerable perception of effort

### Leverage Differences by Muscle Region
- **Lats (latissimus dorsi)**: Upper/thoracic region has better leverage in sagittal plane pulling; lower/lumbar region has better leverage in frontal plane
- **Pecs**: Different regions have varying leverage based on grip width and torso angle
- **Shoulders**: Anterior deltoid has good leverage in frontal plane abduction; middle deltoid in pure abduction

## Evidence
- Activation data shows upper lat fibers more activated in narrow grip rows; lower lat fibers more activated in wide grip pull-downs
- Bilateral force deficit: Can perform >50% of bilateral load unilaterally due to central motor command limitations
- Maximal voluntary contractions show deficits scaling with muscle mass (2-3% in small muscles like elbow flexors, up to 15-20% in large muscles like quads/glutes)

## Implications for Training
### Exercise Selection
- Multiple exercises per muscle group ensure more complete fiber recruitment
- Single exercises cannot fully train all muscle fibers due to leverage differences
- Example: Wide grip lat pull-down + narrow grip row + pullover for complete lat development

### Training Frequency
- **Full body ABC splits don't work** - fibers would atrophy between sessions
- **Full body AB splits are optimal** - fibers trained every ~5 days stay in growth territory
- **Upper/Lower splits require same exercises** in both sessions - cannot do A/B format

### Volume Distribution
- One set of 3 different exercises can provide stimulus similar to 3 sets of one exercise for overlapping fibers
- Unique fibers get additional stimulus from varied exercises
- Raises ceiling on muscular potential compared to single exercise focus
