# MYOPS Elevation Correction

## Definition
The concept that elevated myofibrillar protein synthesis (MYOPS) rates from a previous workout may slightly reduce the hypertrophy stimulus available for a subsequent workout, with an estimated reduction of 10-20% at most.

## Scientific Basis
Based on analysis of two key studies (Damas review and Bird study), when MYOPS rates are still elevated at the time of a subsequent workout:

- The cellular machinery that synthesizes proteins has a fixed capacity
- If previous workout repair processes are using 10-20% of this capacity, only 80-90% remains for the new stimulus
- This is a capacity limitation, not an absolute block on hypertrophy

## Evidence
- Damas review (muscle protein synthesis rates) shows MPS peaks at ~160% above baseline after 6-8 hours, then declines to ~10-15% by ~36-48 hours
- Bird study shows single set returns to baseline at ~36 hours, three sets at ~60 hours
- For higher volume programs (6-12 sets), the correction is closer to 10%
- For moderate volume (3 sets), the correction is closer to 15-20%

## Practical Applications
- The correction only applies to multi-set programs training 3x per week or every other day
- Single set programs are unaffected - MYOPS returns to baseline within 36 hours
- Two set programs likely return to baseline around 48 hours
- Even with the correction, 3x per week still outperforms 2x per week due to the larger volume differential
- The weekly net stimulus model can incorporate this correction (user choice of 10%, 15%, or 20% reduction)

## Key Insights
- MYOPS elevation alone does not equal hypertrophy stimulus period
- Protein synthesis can be elevated without net protein accretion (e.g., from protein feeding alone, aerobic exercise, or damage repair)
- The effect is minimal and should not deter higher frequency training with moderate volumes
