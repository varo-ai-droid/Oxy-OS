# Neural Adaptation

## Definition
Changes in the nervous system that improve the ability to recruit motor units, increase firing rate, and coordinate muscle activation. Neural adaptations are the primary driver of strength gains in the early stages of training.

## Key Mechanisms
- **Increased motor unit recruitment**: More motor units are activated for a given effort level
- **Increased firing rate**: Motor units fire more rapidly, producing more force
- **Improved coordination**: Better sequencing of muscle activation across joints
- **Reduced antagonistic co-contraction**: Less opposition from opposing muscle groups

## Timeline
- Neural adaptations dominate strength gains in the first 4-8 weeks of training
- After this period, hypertrophy becomes the primary driver of continued strength increases
- Neural adaptations can occur rapidly, often within a single training session
