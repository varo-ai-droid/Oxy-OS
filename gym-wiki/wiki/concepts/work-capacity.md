# Work Capacity

## Definition
From a hypertrophy perspective, work capacity is defined as the ability to perform more work (sets x reps x load) with a current level of maximum strength - essentially "fatigue resistance" at a given percentage of 1RM.

## Types of Work Capacity

### Recoverable Volume
- Amount of work you can do in a workout and recover from by the next planned workout
- Muscle-specific, not systemic
- Increases slightly with repeated bout effect (calcium ion related fatigue mechanisms)

### Absolute Work Capacity
- Combination of maximum strength + fatigue resistance
- Measured as total weight lifted in a fixed time period (volume load)
- Not particularly relevant for hypertrophy adaptation

### Relative Work Capacity
- Fatigue resistance independent of strength gains
- Measured as reps performed at a given percentage of 1RM
- Only adaptation that has limited lifespan (~4 weeks)

## Physiological Basis

### Fatigue Resistance Adaptations
- **Metabolite-related**: Improved buffering capacity, better ability to handle metabolite accumulation
- **Calcium ion-related**: Reduced post-workout fatigue through repeated bout effect
- **Supraspinal CNS**: Improved tolerance to painful sensations (cardiovascular/metabolite)

### Primary Driver
- **Rep range** is the primary driver of fatigue resistance changes
- Light loads (15+ reps) produce significant fatigue resistance adaptations
- Moderate loads (4-8 reps) produce minimal fatigue resistance changes
- Heavy loads (1-3 reps) show no significant change

## Programming Implications

### For Hypertrophy
- Increased work capacity does NOT directly enhance hypertrophy stimulus
- Metabolite accumulation doesn't affect hypertrophy stimulus as long as it doesn't cause supraspinal CNS fatigue
- The calcium ion related fatigue mechanisms affect mechanical tension capability

### For Daily Training
- Higher rep ranges and fatigue resistance help with daily training recovery
- But still limited by the ~1-1.5 sets recoverable per day with moderate loads
- Heavy loads allow more repetitions without fatigue due to faster recovery

## Methods to Improve
- **Light load training** (15+ reps): Significant fatigue resistance improvements
- **Long slow cardio**: Improves cardiovascular capacity for clearing metabolites between sets
- **Volume**: Has minimal effect on fatigue resistance (contrary to popular belief)
