# Progressive Overload

## Definition
Progressive overload is the gradual increase of stress placed upon the body during exercise to drive adaptation. The fundamental principle that continued improvement requires increased demands over time.

## Scientific Basis
- Adaptation occurs in response to stress; when stress remains constant, adaptation stops
- Requires systematic increase in one or more training variables
- Recovery must match or exceed stress to enable adaptation

## Evidence
- Essential for continued progress across all training goals
- Systematic review shows progressive overload as core training principle [[Kraemer & Ratamess 2004]]
- Can be achieved via load, reps, sets, or improved technique

## Practical Applications
- Small, consistent progressions beat sporadic large jumps
- Track your workouts to ensure progression
- When stuck, vary the overload variable (try more reps instead of more weight)
