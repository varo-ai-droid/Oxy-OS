# Supraspinal Fatigue

## Definition
A fatigue mechanism mediated by the brain's perception of effort, where sensations from cardiovascular activity, metabolite accumulation, and inflammation bring the individual closer to their maximum tolerable perception of effort, thereby limiting motor unit recruitment.

## Scientific Basis
Based on the psycho-biological model of fatigue:

### Within-Session Supraspinal Fatigue
Occurs during workouts due to:
- **Cardiovascular activity**: Perception of breathlessness and lung sensation
- **Metabolite accumulation**: Burning sensations in muscles during exercise
- **Inflammation response**: Lactate-induced systemic inflammation creating feelings of fatigue

The underlying mechanism: When effort availability is used to tolerate discomforting sensations, less is available for motor unit recruitment, even at maximal effort attempts.

### Post-Workout Supraspinal Fatigue
Occurs after workouts due to:
- **Muscle damage processes**: Calpain-mediated damage and inflammation responses
- Damage occurs post-workout, not during, with the inflammatory response peaking in the second 24-hour period
- This creates sensations that limit subsequent workout performance

## Physiological Evidence
- Cardiovascular fitness directly impacts within-session fatigue
- Higher cardiovascular capacity allows for more sets per session
- Bilateral force deficit proves voluntary activation deficits exist
- Maximal voluntary contractions show deficits increase with muscle mass involved

## Practical Applications
- Monitor cardiovascular recovery with heart rate monitoring between sets
- People with poor cardiovascular fitness may benefit from splitting full body into upper/lower sessions
- Those prone to muscle damage may benefit from full body sessions with rest days between
- Exercise order matters: do cardiovascularly demanding exercises when fresh

## Assessment Methods
- Heart rate monitoring during workout to assess cardiovascular recovery
- Performance comparison between same-day split vs sequential day training
- Moving exercises from beginning to end of session to test within-session fatigue impact
