# Linear Periodization

## Definition
A training approach where volume decreases and intensity increases over time within a training cycle. Typically involves moving from higher reps/lower weight to lower reps/higher weight across several weeks or months.

## Characteristics
- **Early phase**: Higher volume (sets and reps), lower intensity (% of 1RM)
- **Late phase**: Lower volume, higher intensity
- **Goal**: Build work capacity and muscle mass early, then peak strength later

## For Hypertrophy
- Limited value for hypertrophy compared to simpler approaches
- The recoverable volume window (1-3 sets per workout) makes volume periodization impractical
- Periodizing volume upward causes fatigue accumulation that outweighs any hypertrophy benefit
- Simpler plans often perform as well or better than complex periodization for hypertrophy

## Research Context
- Undulating periodization shows slight advantages over linear (Rhea et al. 2002)
- Block periodization effective for advanced athletes (Issurin 2008)
- For most trainees, consistent progressive overload within a simple program is sufficient
