# Stretch Under Load

## Definition
Stretch-mediated hypertrophy occurs when muscle fibers are stretched while under tension, triggering unique signaling pathways (titin, focal adhesion kinase) distinct from metabolic stress or traditional mechanical tension.

## Physiological Mechanisms

### Titin Pathway
- Titin protein senses muscle elongation under load
- Triggers signaling independent of mTOR
- Can add sarcomeres in series (longer muscle fibers)
- May cause myofibril atrophy in stretch-mediated protocols

### Focal Adhesion Kinase
- Mechanosensitive pathway activated by stretch
- Works alongside titin for hypertrophy signaling
- Distinct from traditional tension-mediated pathways
- Research shows stretch + load superior to load alone

## Evidence

### Research Findings
- Pedrosa et al. 2022: Stretch under load enhances adaptations
- Flywheel/eccentric overload studies support concept
- Blood flow restriction with stretch shows additive effects
- Heavy loads with stretch may maximize this pathway

### Calcium Ion Connection
- Stretch increases calcium ion accumulation
- More damaging effects with large ROM exercises
- Tradeoff: stretch-mediated growth vs increased fatigue
- Range of motion considerations for multiple sets

## Applications

### Exercise Selection
- Pullovers and pullover variations
- Overhead pressing with deep stretch
- Hip flexor stretches under load
- Flywheel/cable exercises with peak contraction at stretch

### Programming Considerations
- May require reduced volume due to fatigue
- Combines with mechanical tension for maximal effect
- Stretch-mediated MPS may not equal growth
- Consider for muscle groups needing size over density
