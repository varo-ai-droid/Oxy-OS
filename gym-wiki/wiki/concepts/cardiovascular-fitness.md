# Cardiovascular Fitness

## Definition
The ability of the heart, lungs, and circulatory system to deliver oxygen to working muscles during sustained physical activity. Cardiovascular fitness affects recovery between sets, work capacity, and overall training performance.

## Relationship to Strength Training

### Impact on Recovery
- Higher cardiovascular fitness improves inter-set recovery
- Reduces breathlessness and perception of effort between sets
- Allows shorter rest periods without compromising performance

### Impact on Supraspinal Fatigue
- Cardiovascular sensations (breathlessness, elevated heart rate) contribute to supraspinal CNS fatigue
- Better cardiovascular fitness reduces these sensations, potentially reducing fatigue perception
- This is particularly relevant for multi-joint exercises with high systemic demand

### Training Considerations
- Excessive cardio can interfere with hypertrophy if it creates additional systemic fatigue
- Low-intensity cardio generally has minimal interference with strength gains
- High-intensity interval training may compete with recovery from strength training
