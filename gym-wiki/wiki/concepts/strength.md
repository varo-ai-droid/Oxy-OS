# Strength

## Definition
Strength is the maximal force a muscle or muscle group can generate. Strength gains primarily result from neural adaptations in the early phases, with hypertrophy becoming more significant over longer periods.

## Scientific Basis
Neural adaptations include:
- Improved motor unit recruitment
- Enhanced rate coding (firing frequency)
- Better inter-muscular coordination
- Reduced antagonist co-activation

## Evidence
- Neural adaptations dominate early strength gains (first 4-8 weeks)
- Hypertrophy becomes primary contributor after ~8 weeks
- Heavy loads (85-95% 1RM) are optimal for maximal strength

## Practical Applications
- Lower rep ranges (1-5) for strength-specific training
- Longer rest periods (2-5 minutes)
- Longer adaptation periods needed
- Progressive overload via increased load rather than volume
