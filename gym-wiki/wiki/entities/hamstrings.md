# Hamstrings

## Definition
A muscle group consisting of three muscles on the posterior thigh: biceps femoris (long and short heads), semitendinosus, and semimembranosus. Responsible for knee flexion and hip extension.

## Anatomy
- **Biceps Femoris (Long Head)**: Laterally positioned, hip extension, knee flexion
- **Biceps Femoris (Short Head)**: Medially originates, primarily knee flexion
- **Semitendinosus**: Medially positioned, hip extension, knee flexion
- **Semimembranosus**: Deep medial muscle, hip extension, knee flexion

## Regional Considerations
- Upper (proximal) region vs lower (distal) region
- Neuromechanical matching affects regional recruitment
- Hip extension emphasis recruits all heads
- Knee flexion emphasis prioritizes short heads

## Training Approaches
- **Stiff Leg Deadlift**: Hip hinge with stretch-under-load
- **Romanian Deadlift**: Similar hip-dominant pattern
- **Lying Leg Curl**: Direct isolation for knee flexion
- **Good Morning**: Eccentric emphasis on hip extensors

## Historical Context
- Rarely trained directly in silver era
- Undertrained muscle group in historical routines
- Machine-based isolation came later
- Often relied on compound movements (deadlifts) for stimulus
