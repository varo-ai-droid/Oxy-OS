# Arm Training

## Definition
Focused training approaches targeting biceps and triceps development, often requiring specialized programming due to their smaller size and frequent involvement in compound movements.

## Physiological Considerations

### Frequency Needs
- Arms receive significant stimulus in back (biceps) and chest (triceps) training
- Direct arm work may be necessary for complete development
- Specialized phases often require dedicated arm volume

### Exercise Selection
- Biceps: Elbow flexion variations, underhand pulling
- Triceps: Elbow extension variations, overhead positions
- Long head of triceps: Requires overhead/arm position
- Multiple angles for complete regional development

### Volume Considerations
- Smaller muscle groups can handle high frequency
- Direct arm work can supplement compound movements
- Specialization phases: High frequency (daily), low volume

## Historical Context
- [[Chuck Sipes]] pioneer of arm specialization phases
- Silver era often included direct arm work in full body routines
- Specialization phases used for lagging muscle groups
