# Romanian Deadlift

## Description
A deadlift variation emphasizing hip flexion with minimal knee bend, targeting the hamstrings and glutes through stretch-under-load tension. More modern term for stiff leg deadlift variations.

## Form Cues
- Start with feet hip-width, bar close to shins
- Hinge at hips, maintaining slight knee bend (micro-bend)
- Lower bar down legs until stretch felt in hamstrings
- Push hips forward to return, avoiding excessive knee drive
- Maintain neutral spine throughout

## Relationship to Stiff Leg Deadlift
- Romanian Deadlift is the modern terminology for stiff leg variations
- Both emphasize hip-dominant hamstring loading
- RDL typically uses more knee flexion than stiff leg
- Both predate dedicated hamstring curl machines

## Historical Context
- Contemporary of stiff leg deadlift in silver era
- Used as alternative to back squats for posterior chain
- Emerged before machine-based hamstring isolation exercises

## Evidence
- Part of silver era programs achieving significant development
- Preferred for targeted hamstring work in historical routines
- Less quadriceps interference than conventional deadlift
