# C. Klein

## Description
Physical culture figure from the late 1920s through early 1940s, bridging the "bronze era" and "silver era" of bodybuilding. Known for promoting an AB full body training structure despite training once daily being uncommon at the time.

## Historical Context
- Active in the pre-1950s period before steroid availability
- Competed in physique competitions, winning titles including "Most Muscular Man in America" and "World's Best Man"
- Married to the daughter of Professor Attila, who was Eugene Sandow's mentor
- Connected to the silver era bodybuilders through family ties

## Training Contributions
- Published beginner workout plans using AB split structure (alternating two different full body workouts)
- Advocated single set training with 12-15 reps range initially, progressing to 8 reps with heavier load
- Emphasized training each muscle 3 times per week through alternating full body routines

## Key Workout Elements
- AB full body structure with alternating exercises between sessions
- Single set per exercise approach (pre-dating popular set system adoption)
- Progressive rep range manipulation: 12-15 reps → 8 reps with increased load
- Early recognition of exercise variation importance (different exercises on A vs B days)

## Significance
Represented a transitional figure in physical culture who recognized the value of alternating stimulus patterns within full body training, predating modern understanding of training frequency and recovery.
