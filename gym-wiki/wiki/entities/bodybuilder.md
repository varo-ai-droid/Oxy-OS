# Bodybuilder

## Definition
Category for individuals who competed in or specialized in bodybuilding physique development. In the context of this wiki, focused on pre-steroid era (silver and golden era) bodybuilders who developed training methods without anabolic assistance.

## Historical Context
- **Silver Era** (pre-1958): Bodybuilders before widespread anabolic steroid availability
- **Golden Era** (late 1950s-1970s): Transition period with emerging steroid use
- Bodybuilders of these eras provide insight into effective training practices independent of pharmacological enhancement

## Notable Figures
- [[Chuck Sipes]] - Known for cluster-style training, specialization phases
- [[Bill Pearl]] - Represented transition period into steroid era
- [[Dorian Yates]] - Later era but built on golden era foundations
- [[Steve Reeves]] - Mr. America winner, advocate of exercise variety
- [[Reg Park]] - Mr. Universe winner, known for 5x5 training system
- [[George Hackenschmidt]] - Early 20th century physical culture pioneer

## Significance
- Pre-steroid era bodybuilders had clear feedback on training effectiveness
- Their programs demonstrate effectiveness of high-volume approaches
- Recovery appeared accelerated compared to natural trainees today
- Provides historical foundation for modern hypertrophy understanding

## Training Characteristics (Historical)
- High volume per muscle group (5-10+ sets in some programs)
- Split routines became common in golden era
- Successive training days often used
- 5-6 day per week training frequency common

## Evidence Base
- Programs documented through magazines ([[Iron Man Magazine]])
- Competition results and physique development
- Training philosophies recorded in courses and articles
