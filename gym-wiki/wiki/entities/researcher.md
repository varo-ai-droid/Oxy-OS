# Researcher

## Definition
Entity group for researchers and scientific contributors to exercise physiology and strength training. In the context of this wiki, focused on historical figures who documented training methodologies before modern scientific validation.

## Notable Figures
- [[C. Klein]] - Bronze/silver era transitional figure, AB split pioneer
- [[Joe Weider]] - Founder of IFBB, early advocate of full body then split training
- [[George Hackenschmidt]] - Early 20th century physical culture icon promoting daily training
- [[Chuck Sipes]] - Golden era bodybuilder, pioneer of arm specialization and cluster-style training
- [[Piri Radar]] - Iron Man magazine founder, advocate of specialization phases

## Historical Context
These individuals were physical culture figures and early researchers who:
- Developed training methodologies through practical application
- Published courses and plans before modern peer-reviewed research
- Bridged the gap between empirical observation and scientific understanding
- Provided clear feedback on training effectiveness (pre-steroid era)

## Research Contributions
- Documented training methods and principles
- Identified effective approaches for muscle development
- Created periodization concepts before scientific validation
- Established the foundation for modern hypertrophy understanding

## Significance
- Historical researchers and coaches who developed training methodologies before modern scientific validation
- Their programs provide insight into effective training practices independent of anabolic assistance
- Shows physiology-consistent approaches developed through trial and error
- Provides baseline understanding of what works without pharmacological enhancement

## Evidence Base
- Training courses published through magazines like [[Iron Man Magazine]]
- Competition results and physique development documentation
- Methodologies that align with modern physiological understanding
