# Joe Weider

## Description
Founder of the Weider organization and magazine, one of the most influential figures in bodybuilding history. Known for popularizing the set system and split training routines, though early in his career he promoted full body training.

## Historical Context
- Founded the International Federation of BodyBuilders (IFBB)
- Major competitor to Bob Hoffman's York Barbell
- Mentor to Arnold Schwarzenegger among other notable bodybuilders
- Marketed "Weider Principles" training system
- Promoted split routines and high volume training in later career

## Early Training Philosophy (Pre-1950)
- Published full body routines in early magazine issues (1940s)
- Advocated single set training with 5-10 rep range
- Three times per week full body training structure
- Focused on heavy to moderately heavy loads (1-5 to 6-10 rep ranges)

## Training Contributions
- Popularized bodybuilding split routines (upper/lower, push/pull, etc.)
- Advocated for the set system in strength training
- Emphasized varied exercise selection for complete development
- Created a bodybuilding empire that influenced training worldwide

## Notable Programs
- Early full body routines with single sets, 3x per week
- Later developed the "Weider System" of split training
- Influenced the transition from strength-focused to physique-focused training
