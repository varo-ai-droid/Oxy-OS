# Shoulders

## Definition
Shoulder muscle group consisting of three heads (anterior, medial/middle, posterior deltoid) that primarily control arm movement in the shoulder joint. Often trained as part of upper body pushing and pulling movements.

## Anatomy
- **Anterior Deltoid**: Front head, involved in shoulder flexion and transverse flexion
- **Lateral/Medial Deltoid**: Middle head, primary abduction of the arm
- **Posterior Deltoid**: Rear head, involved in shoulder extension and transverse extension

## Training Considerations
- Overhead pressing was emphasized in early silver era programs
- Wide grip variations emphasize anterior deltoid and clavicular head
- Frontal plane exercises (lateral raises) target middle deltoid
- Rear deltoid often undertrained in traditional bodybuilding routines

## Primary Exercises
- [[Behind the Neck Press]] - Overhead pressing variation
- [[Wide Grip Bench Press]] - Can emphasize anterior deltoid with clavicular pec
- [[Upright Row]] - Vertical pulling for upper traps and delts
- [[Vertical Shrug]] - Upper trap emphasis with shoulder girdle elevation
- Lateral raises (dumbbell, cable)
- Rear delt fly variations

## Neuromechanical Matching
- Overhead pressing emphasized in early routines due to leverage advantages
- Frontal plane work (lateral raises) provides different activation patterns
- Different grip widths change which deltoid regions are prioritized
- Narrow grip bench press may target anterior deltoid differently than wide grip

## Historical Context
- Early silver era bodybuilders emphasized overhead pressing for shoulder development
- Wide grip bench press commonly used to target sternal pec and anterior deltoid
- Shoulder development was aesthetic goal in pre-1950s era
- Shift occurred from overhead pressing focus to other shoulder exercises over time

## Evidence
- Regional variation in shoulder activation based on exercise selection
- Frontal plane exercises may provide superior middle deltoid activation
- Overhead pressing creates high mechanical tension for deltoid fibers
- Recovery considerations for deltoids in full body vs split routines
