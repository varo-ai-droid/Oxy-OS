# Stiff Leg Deadlift

## Description
A deadlift variation performed with minimal knee flexion, emphasizing hip flexion and elongation. Developed by silver era bodybuilders as a hamstring-focused exercise before dedicated hamstring curl machines existed.

## Form Cues
- Keep knees nearly straight (slight micro-bend) throughout the movement
- Hinge at the hips, pushing glutes back
- Maintain neutral spine, avoid rounding
- Lower until stretch felt in hamstrings (typically mid-shin to ankle level)
- Drive hips forward to return, avoiding knee drive

## Variations
- [[Romanian Deadlift]] - More modern term, similar execution
- Seated barbell good morning - Alternative for hamstrings with reduced quadriceps interference

## Historical Context
- Featured in Clancy Ross's Mr. America routine (1940s)
- Early acknowledgment of hamstring training specificity before machine-based isolation
- Mentioned in Steve Reeves's programs as part of posterior chain development

## Evidence
- Part of silver era bodybuilding programs that achieved significant development without anabolic assistance
- Preferred over conventional deadlift for targeted hamstring development in historical routines
