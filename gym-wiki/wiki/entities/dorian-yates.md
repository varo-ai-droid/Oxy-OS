# Dorian Yates

## Description
British professional bodybuilder and 6-time Mr. Olympia winner (1992-1997), known for "Mass Monster" physique and "Blood and Guts" training philosophy emphasizing high-intensity, low-volume training.

## Training Philosophy
- **Blood and Guts**: Training to complete failure with heavy loads
- **Low volume, high intensity**: Focused on quality over quantity
- **Torso/Limb AB Split**: 3x/week training with specialized splits

## Notable Training Approach (Pre-1985)
- **Workout Structure**: Torso vs Limb AB split, 3 workouts per week
- **Workout A (Torso)**: Chest, back, delts, abdominals
- **Workout B (Limbs)**: Quads, hamstrings, calves, arms
- **Set Structure**: 1-3 sets of 8 reps per exercise, increasing load when 8 reps achieved with 1RIR

## Key Insights
- Stuck to same exercises for years, contrary to "change every 4-8 weeks" mentality
- Showed "extraordinary instinctual understanding" of recruitment principles
- His approach inadvertently validated physiological models despite lacking scientific knowledge

## Influence
- Popularized high-intensity training in modern bodybuilding
- Bridge between golden era instincts and modern physiological understanding
- Demonstrated effectiveness of consistent, high-effort training over varied programming
