# Golden Era

## Definition
Period of bodybuilding history spanning roughly 1950s-1970s when anabolic steroids became available but were not yet widely used. Characterized by high-volume training approaches and split routines.

## Historical Context
- Begins with introduction of anabolic steroids (~1958-1960s)
- Coincides with rise of high-volume training methods
- Features transition from full body to split routines
- Popularized by Weider methods and Arnold Schwarzenegger
- Bridge between silver era (pre-steroid) and modern bodybuilding

## Training Characteristics
- High volume per muscle group (5-10+ sets)
- Split routines (upper/lower, push/pull/legs)
- Successive training days common
- Often involved 5-6 day per week training frequency
- Emphasis on progressive overload and variety

## Key Figures
- [[Chuck Sipes]] - Known for cluster-style training, specialization phases
- [[Bill Pearl]] - Represented transition period into steroid era
- [[Dorian Yates]] - Later era but built on golden era foundations
- [[Steve Reeves]] - Mr. America winner, advocate of exercise variety
- [[Reg Park]] - Mr. Universe winner, known for 5x5 training

## Training Method Evolution
- Development of the set system (gradual increase to multiple sets)
- Introduction of specialization phases
- Cluster set principles for heavy loading
- Split routine structures becoming mainstream

## Significance
- Provides contrast to silver era (pre-steroid) approaches
- Shows impact of anabolic assistance on training methodology
- Recovery appears accelerated, enabling higher volumes
- Feedback loop breaking affects program evaluation
- Programs from this era documented in [[Iron Man Magazine]] and other publications
