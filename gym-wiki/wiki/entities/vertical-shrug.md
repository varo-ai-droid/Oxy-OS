# Vertical Shrug

## Definition
Silver era trapezius exercise performed standing with a barbell, emphasizing vertical elevation of the shoulder girdle to target the upper trapezius fibers.

## Execution
- Stand upright holding barbell at shoulder width
- Elevate shoulder girdle vertically (shrug up, not back/forward)
- Hold at top for 1-2 seconds
- Lower under control to starting position
- Pure vertical movement pattern

## Physiological Characteristics
- **Fiber Recruitment**: Upper trapezius emphasis
- **Movement Pattern**: Vertical axis, different from lateral raises
- **Neuromechanical Matching**: Provides different fiber recruitment than other trap exercises
- **Leverage**: Good leverage through full range of motion when performed correctly

## Historical Usage
- Featured in Bob Hoffman's York Barbell courses (1930s-1950s)
- Complemented cleans and other pulling movements
- Provided upper trap development in minimal-equipment programs

## Programming Notes
- Part of silver era full body routines
- Often used as finisher for shoulder girdle
- Complements pulling exercises (rows, pull-ups) for complete trap development
- Simple exercise with high stability
