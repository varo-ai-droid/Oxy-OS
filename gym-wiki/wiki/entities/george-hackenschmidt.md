# George Hackenschmidt

## Overview
Early 20th century physical culture icon (published 1900s-1910s) who invented the hack squat. Professional wrestler known as the "Russian Lion" who achieved significant muscular development without modern equipment or pharmaceuticals.

## Training Philosophy
- **Frequency**: Trained most days (5-7 days per week)
- **Volume**: Single set per exercise
- **Progression**: Start with 10RM, perform 5 reps, add 1 rep each workout until reaching 10, then add weight and restart
- **Workout Duration**: ~20-30 minutes
- **Load**: Moderate loads (10RM range)

## Notable Contributions
- Hack squat exercise (barbell variation, not machine)
- Wrestler's bridge exercise
- One-arm press variations
- Philosophy of daily training with controlled stimulus

## Program Design Principles
Hackenschmidt's program demonstrates an early understanding of:
- Managing post-workout fatigue by avoiding failure (missing last 2 reps)
- Using moderate loads to stay within daily recoverable stimulus
- Progressive overload through rep progression before load increases

## Relevance to Modern Training
His approach provides historical evidence that daily training can work when:
- Stimulus is kept low (avoiding muscle damage)
- Each exercise provides only a few stimulating reps per day
- Recovery is prioritized through load management
