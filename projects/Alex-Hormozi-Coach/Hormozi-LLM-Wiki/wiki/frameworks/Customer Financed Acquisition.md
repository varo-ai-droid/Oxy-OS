# Customer Financed Acquisition (CFA)

## Definition
**Customer Financed Acquisition** is the strategy of using customer revenue to fund your customer acquisition activities. Instead of paying upfront costs for marketing, you use the initial customer payment to cover acquisition expenses, removing cash constraints on growth.

## The Three Levers of CFA

### 1. Cost To Acquire A Customer (CAC)
**Your profit is a function of how much it costs to acquire a customer.**

Lower CAC means:
- More budget for scaling
- Faster breakeven
- Higher margins to reinvest

Ways to reduce CAC:
- Better targeting using [[Customer Avatar]]
- Compelling [[Attraction Offers]] that convert naturally
- Faster [[Dream Outcome]] delivery to generate referrals

### 2. Lifetime Gross Profit (LTGP)
**Maximize the total profit you'll receive from a customer over their entire relationship.**

Higher LTGP allows:
- More to spend on acquisition
- Better customer experience investments
- Sustainable competitive advantages

Ways to increase LTGP:
- [[Upsell Offers]] to increase transaction value
- [[Continuity Offers]] for recurring revenue
- Extended customer relationships and retention

### 3. Payback Period (PPD)
**Time between acquisition cost and recouping that investment.**

Shorter PPD means:
- Faster scaling ability
- Less upfront capital required
- Quicker validation of strategies

Ways to improve PPD:
- [[Guarantees]] that build trust (reducing refunds)
- Faster [[Dream Outcome]] delivery
- Upfront payment structures

## The CFA Equation

```
CAC = (LTGP / PPD) × Multiple of Acquisition Spend
```

Understanding this relationship helps optimize your customer acquisition strategy.

## Levels of Customer Financed Acquisition

### Basic Level
- Single transaction, no follow-up
- Limited profit per customer
- Longer payback period

### Intermediate Level
- Some upsells or continuity
- Moderate profit optimization
- Medium payback period

### Advanced Level
- Full [[Money Models]] implementation
- High LTGP through stacking
- Short payback period through trust-building
- [[Offer Stacking]] for maximum value

## Key Principles

### Profit Per Customer Matters
The more profit you make per customer, the more you can spend to acquire them.

### Time is Money
Shorter payback periods mean faster growth and less financial stress.

### Acquisition Precedes Cash Flow
Good CFA means getting paid before you spend, not after.
