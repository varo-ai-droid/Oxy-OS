# Core Four

## Definition
The **Core Four** is Alex Hormozi's framework for the four essential ways to let people know about a business or product. All lead generation strategies fall into these four categories.

## The Four Core Lead Generation Methods

### 1. Warm Reachouts
Direct personal outreach to audiences who already know you or have some familiarity.

**Key Strategies:**
- 10-step process for warm reachouts
- Personalized messaging and offers
- Leverage existing relationships and networks

**Best For:** Building on existing trust, immediate connections

### 2. Content Posting
Publishing valuable content to attract and educate potential customers.

**Key Strategies:**
- Consistently publish (Rule of 100: 1 piece/day)
- Provide value before asking for anything
- Build authority in your niche

**Best For:** Long-term brand building, organic growth

### 3. Cold Reachouts
Direct outreach to people who don't know you yet.

**Key Strategies:**
- Systematic outreach campaigns
- Rule of 100: 100 reachouts per day (email, text, DM, calls, flyers)
- Focus on solving their problems

**Best For:** Testing new markets, quick validation

### 4. Paid Ads
Paid advertising to reach targeted audiences.

**Key Strategies:**
- Continuous split testing
- Focus on front-end elements (headlines, CTAs, landing pages)
- Rule of 100: 100 minutes per day creating/managing ads
- Client-financed acquisition models

**Best For:** Scalable growth, precise targeting

## More Better New Strategy

### Progression Principle
When scaling lead generation, follow this order:

1. **More** - Increase volume of current efforts
2. **Better** - Improve quality and effectiveness of existing methods
3. **New** - Expand into new areas only when returns diminish

### Business Size Matters

**Under $1M Profit:** Focus on **More**
- Volume drives results at this stage
- Simple increases compound quickly

**Over $1M Profit:** Focus on **Better**
- Quality improvements become most cost-effective
- Optimization yields higher returns than pure volume

### Expansion Order
When pursuing "New" strategies:
1. New ad placements on known platforms
2. Familiar placements on new platforms
3. New placements on new platforms
4. New core activities (different lead gen method entirely)

## The Size of the Pie Fallacy
The misconception that market size is limited to current platforms and competitors. In reality, the market is often much larger when you:
- Expand to new platforms
- Improve your offer quality
- Target underserved segments

## Key Principles

### Compounding Effects
All four methods compound and support each other when combined. Master one method first, then expand systematically.

### Constraints
Find where leads drop off in your funnel (opt-in rates, application rates, scheduling rates) and prioritize improving the biggest constraint.

### Front-End Focus
The biggest leverage in paid ads comes from optimizing front-end elements first.
