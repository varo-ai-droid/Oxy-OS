# Value Equation

## The Fundamental Formula

```
(Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice) = Value
```

This equation determines how much value a prospect perceives in your offer. Understanding this allows you to craft offers that make people feel stupid saying no.

## The Four Value Drivers

### Dream Outcome (↑ Increase)
People have deep, unchanging desires: to be beautiful, respected, powerful, loved, increase status. Your offer should channel these existing desires.

**Key Insight:** The dream outcome that most directly increases a prospect's status is generally valued the most. Frame benefits in terms of status gained from others' perspectives.

**Example:** "Your golf buddy's jaws will drop" - focusing on external validation/status.

### Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (↑ Increase)
People pay for certainty. How likely do they believe they will achieve the result?

**Ways to Increase:**
- Messaging that builds confidence
- Proof (testimonials, case studies, track record)
- Guarantees
- What you include/exclude in your offer

**Example:** A plastic surgeon's 10,000th patient is perceived as more valuable than their first, even with identical service.

### Time Delay (↓ Decrease)
The time between purchase and receiving the promised benefit. The shorter this distance, the more valuable the offer.

**Strategies:**
- Create "emotional wins" fast and early
- "Fast Beats Free" - people will pay for speed
- Provide milestones along the journey

**Example:** Getting a client's ads live and first $2,000 sale within 7 days reinforces their decision.

### Effort & Sacrifice (↓ Decrease)
The tangible and intangible costs: time lost, physical discomfort, mental discomfort, hunger, fear, etc.

**Strategies:**
- "Done-for-you" services eliminate effort
- Highlight what your offer removes
- Make it easy and effortless

**Example:** Liposuction (fall asleep, wake up thin, sore 2-4 weeks) vs. fitness regimen (wake early, diet, meal prep, etc.) for same outcome.

## Key Principles

### Perception is Reality
Value is only created once the prospect *perceives* the increase/decrease in the four variables. Not enough to actually improve; must be *communicated* effectively.

**Example:** London Tube's dotted map (costing millions) increased rider satisfaction more than faster trains (costing billions) by reducing the *perception* of wait time.

### Logical vs Psychological Solutions
Most default to logical solutions (make product cheaper, faster). These are often already tried and failed.

Psychological solutions work better: mirrors in elevators, make fewer products to increase desire, pay models to be hostesses on a trip to make people wish it took longer.

### Monetize Value, Deliver Needs
Understand what people *value* (convenience, speed) even if it's not what's "good" for them.

**Example:** Supplements ($123B industry) vs. gyms ($62B) - pills perceived as faster/easier than gym visits.
