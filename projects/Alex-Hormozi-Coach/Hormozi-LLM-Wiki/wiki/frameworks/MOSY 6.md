# MOSY 6 Framework

## Definition
The **MOSY 6 Framework** is Alex Hormozi's diagnostic tool for identifying business constraints and determining which lever to pull next to maximize output. It stands for **Market, Offer, Sales & Traffic, Systems, Yield & People, Operations** - the six fundamental components of any business.

## The Six Components

### 1. Market
**Focus:** Selecting the right niche and audience

**Key Questions:**
- Is your market growing?
- Does it have purchasing power?
- Is it easy to target?
- Is there massive, urgent demand?

**Diagnosis:** If sales are stagnant despite effort, the market might be too small, not hungry enough, or difficult to reach.

**Solutions:**
- Find a starving crowd with inherent demand
- Target underserved segments
- Expand to new platforms/markets

### 2. Offer
**Focus:** Creating an irresistible value proposition

**Key Questions:**
- Can customers achieve their dream outcome?
- How likely do they feel they'll succeed?
- How much time and effort is required?
- Does it feel like a steal at this price?

**Diagnosis:** If the market is suitable but conversion rates are low, the offer may not be compelling or its value isn't clearly communicated.

**Solutions:**
- Apply the Value Equation to increase perceived value
- Use offer enhancers (bonuses, guarantees, scarcity)
- Improve delivery method via Delivery Cube
- Raise prices to signal quality

### 3. Sales & Traffic
**Focus:** Generating a predictable stream of potential customers

**Key Questions:**
- How do you let people know about your business?
- What's your lead generation volume?
- How well does your sales process convert?
- Are you reaching decision-makers?

**Diagnosis:** If the market and offer are strong but there's a lack of leads or sales, the problem lies in insufficient traffic generation or ineffective sales processes.

**Solutions:**
- Apply Core Four lead generation methods
- Implement Rule of 100 for consistency
- Create compelling attraction offers
- Train on sales skills and objection handling

### 4. Systems
**Focus:** Documenting repeatable actions and reducing founder dependency

**Key Questions:**
- Can the business run without you?
- Are processes documented?
- Is work delegated effectively?
- Can quality be maintained at scale?

**Diagnosis:** If the business is chaotic, inefficient, or overly reliant on the founder despite incoming sales, the lack of robust systems is the bottleneck.

**Solutions:**
- Document every repeatable process
- Create standard operating procedures
- Automate where possible
- Delegate with clear expectations

### 5. Yield & People
**Focus:** Optimizing efficiency of team and capital

**Key Questions:**
- Are you hiring the right people?
- Is your team productive?
- Are profit margins healthy?
- How well is capital utilized?

**Diagnosis:** If systems are in place but profitability or output per employee is low, the issue is with team efficiency, talent acquisition, or capital utilization.

**Solutions:**
- Apply What-Why-How framework for team management
- Hire for specific constraints
- Optimize pricing and margins
- Improve skill development and training

### 6. Operations
**Focus:** Continuous improvement through data and leak prevention

**Key Questions:**
- Where do customers drop off?
- What are your key KPIs?
- Are you collecting feedback?
- Where are hidden inefficiencies?

**Diagnosis:** If all other areas are addressed but there's still customer churn, poor retention, or hidden inefficiencies, the operational processes need refinement.

**Solutions:**
- Track 3-5 core metrics weekly
- Implement binary scoring (hit/miss)
- Review and iterate regularly
- Optimize customer experience

## Application Process

### Step 1: Identify Your Constraint
Go through each component asking "What's the single biggest constraint?" The constraint is usually in the first 3 areas (Market, Offer, Sales).

### Step 2: Apply One Fix at a Time
Make one change, run for 7 days, measure results. Don't optimize multiple areas simultaneously.

### Step 3: Move to Next Constraint
Once the current constraint shows improvement, identify the next bottleneck. The constraint will "accordion" to a different area.

## The Accordion Effect
As you solve one constraint, the business naturally shifts to the next bottleneck:
- Fixing Offer → Sales becomes constraint
- Fixing Sales → Systems becomes constraint
- Fixing Systems → People/Yield becomes constraint

## Priority Order for Most Businesses

1. **Market** - Get this wrong and nothing else matters
2. **Offer** - Great offers compensate for everything else
3. **Sales & Traffic** - Need customers to prove the model
4. **Systems** - Required for scaling beyond founder
5. **Yield & People** - Optimize once stable
6. **Operations** - Continuous improvement

## Key Questions to Ask

### Market Questions:
- "Would I rather have half my customers with twice the price, or twice my customers with the same price?"
- "Is there a bigger, hungrier market I could serve?"

### Offer Questions:
- "If I paid 10x, what would I expect? If I paid 1/10th, what would I accept?"
- "What problems haven't I solved yet?"

### Sales Questions:
- "Am I doing enough volume to prove execution?"
- "Where am I losing people in the funnel?"
