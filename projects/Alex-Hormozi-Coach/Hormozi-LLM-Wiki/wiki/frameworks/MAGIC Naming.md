# MAGIC Naming

## Definition
The **MAGIC Naming Formula** is Alex Hormozi's headline framework for creating compelling, irresistible offer names. By combining 3-5 psychological elements, you can craft names that capture attention and drive engagement.

## The Five Elements

### M - Magnet (Create an Attractive Theme)
Make your offer more intriguing by creating a special occasion or themed promotion that gives customers a compelling reason to engage.

**Examples:**
- "Winter Solstice Sale"
- "Summer Transformation Challenge"
- "Founder's Club Exclusive"

### A - Avatar (Target Specific Customers)
Tailor your offer's name to resonate with a particular demographic or interest group, making potential customers feel the offer is crafted specifically for them.

**Examples:**
- "Soccer Moms of Waterville" (for lawn chairs)
- "Busy Professional Accelerator"
- "Tech Startup Founder Mastermind"

### G - Goal (Appeal to Deep Desires)
Use words or phrases that evoke your targeted customer's aspirations for success, status, or solutions to their biggest problems.

**Examples:**
- "Effortless Marketing"
- "Double Your Revenue"
- "Confidence Transformation"

### I - Interval (Define the Time Frame)
Be specific about the time frame associated with the offer - either how long it's available or how soon results can be expected.

**Examples:**
- "Memorial Day Weekend Sale"
- "Double Your Clients in Six Months"
- "30-Day Body Transformation"

### C - Container Word (Evoke Uniqueness)
Choose a term that encapsulates the unique nature of your bundle of services or products, suggesting it's not just another commodity.

**Examples:**
- "Booster"
- "Revolution"
- "Blueprint"
- "Formula"
- "System"

## Combined Examples

| Offer Name | Elements Used |
|------------|---------------|
| "10K Club: High Ticket Client Accelerator" | Avatar + Goal + Container + Interval |
| "Luxury Valentine's Escape for Newlyweds" | Magnet + Avatar + Goal |
| "72-Hour Black Friday Cyber Blitz" | Magnet + Interval + Container + Goal |

## Key Principles

### Use 3-5 Elements Per Name
The sweet spot for psychological impact is using 3-5 elements. Too few lacks appeal; too many creates confusion.

### Match to Your Offer Stage
- **Attraction Offers** - Emphasize Magnet, Avatar, Goal
- **Upsell Offers** - Emphasize Value-add, Container
- **Continuity Offers** - Emphasize Interval, Goal

### Test and Iterate
Try different combinations to see which resonates most with your target audience. Track engagement rates for each variant.
