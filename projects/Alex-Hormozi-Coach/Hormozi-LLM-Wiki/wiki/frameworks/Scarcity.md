# Scarcity

## Definition
Scarcity is the intentional limitation of quantity or availability to increase perceived value and demand. It leverages psychological fear of missing out (FOMO) and fear of loss.

## Core Principle
All marketing aims to influence the supply and demand curve:
- Increase demand: Sell more units
- Decrease (perceived) supply: Sell units for more money
- **Perfect Profit Combination:** Lots of demand and very little (or perceived very little) supply

## Scarcity Tactics

### Physical Products - Limited Releases
- Offer limited quantities of specific flavors, colors, designs, or sizes
- Sell out and publicly announce it to create social proof
- Creates desire for future releases

**Example:** Musician drops 100 limited edition hoodies, never to be made again.

### Service-Based Scarcity (Ethical & Real)

#### Total Business Cap
- Only accept a fixed number of clients total (e.g., "My agency only services 25 customers total")
- Create a waiting list when full
- Periodically increase capacity (10-20%) and re-cap

#### Growth Rate Cap
- Limit new clients per period (e.g., "We only accept 5 new clients per week; 3 spots already taken")

#### Cohort Cap
- Limit clients per class or cohort (e.g., "We take on 100 clients four times a year")
- Open doors, then close them

#### Limited Access for High-Ticket Services
- Pair workshops, trainings, events with clear scarcity
- Always have fewer spots than you can sell to ensure selling out

### Extreme Scarcity
- Offer very limited, high-priced one-on-one access
- Cap at tiny numbers (direct message, email, phone, Zoom access)
- **Non-Returnable Access:** If clients leave, they can never return - works best with small groups

### Honest Scarcity
- Simply state real capacity limits (e.g., "We can only handle 10 new clients this month")
- Advertise proximity to capacity (e.g., "81% of capacity in our business")
- Provides social proof and nudges decisions

## Key Insights

### Desire & Unfulfilled Wants
Desire comes from *not* getting what you want. As soon as desire is satisfied, it dissipates. To increase desire, intentionally offer less than the market can absorb, or delay satisfying desires.

**Naval Ravikant Quote:** "Desire is a contract you make with yourself to be unhappy until you get what you want."

### Fear of Loss vs Desire for Gain
Humans are more motivated to act to hoard a scarce resource (fear of loss) than to act on something that could help them (desire for gain).

### Hormozi's Law
"The longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make. The longer the runway, the bigger the plane that can take off."
