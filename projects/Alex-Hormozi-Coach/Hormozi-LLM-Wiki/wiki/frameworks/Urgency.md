# Urgency

## Definition
Urgency is the intentional limitation of time to spur immediate action. It leverages deadlines and time-sensitive decisions to drive purchase behavior.

## Core Principle
Deadlines drive decisions. The majority of sales in time-limited campaigns often occur in the final hours.

## Urgency Tactics

### Cohort-Based Rolling Urgency
- Have regular start dates for clients (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly, quarterly)
- **Strategy:** "If you sign up today, you can join our next group that kicks off on Monday. Otherwise, you'll have to wait until our next kickoff date."

**Handling Missed Cohorts:**
- Offer speedy, personalized onboarding as a bonus
- Explain the advantage of more time to review materials
- Provide extended payment plans

### Rolling Seasonal Urgency
- Run promotions with seasonal themes and defined start/end dates
- **Examples:** "Our New Year promotion ends January 30th" or "Sexy by Spring special ends March 31st"
- **Pro Tip for Local Businesses:** Vary marketing frequently with new seasonal wrappers on core services for novelty and urgency

### Pricing or Bonus-Based Urgency
- Create urgency around temporary discounts or added bonuses
- **Strategy:** "Let's get you started today to take advantage of this discount; I'm not sure how long we'll run it"
- **Pipeline Cleaning:** Announce upcoming price increases to prompt fence-sitters to buy before the change

### Exploding Opportunity
- Emphasize opportunities that decay with time
- Where delay leads to disproportionate losses or missed gains
- **Examples:** Arbitrage opportunities, investing, competitive job offers

## Key Principles

### Hormozi's Law (Time-Based)
"The longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make. The longer the runway, the bigger the plane that can take off."

### Negotiation Principle
The person who needs the exchange less always has the upper hand. Create urgency that makes them feel they might lose the opportunity.
