 # Continuity Offers

## Definition
**Continuity Offers** are recurring revenue models that create predictable, ongoing income from customers. They focus on retaining customers and maximizing lifetime value.

## Why Continuity Works
- Recurring revenue is more predictable than one-time sales
- Lifetime value increases dramatically with good retention
- Customers become more invested over time
- Lower cost to serve existing customers vs acquiring new ones

## The 5 Types of Continuity Offers

### 1. Standard Continuity
**Subscription/recurring models without additional incentives.**

Regular recurring payments for ongoing service or product delivery.

*Example: Monthly software subscription, membership site, coaching program.*

### 2. Continuity with Bonuses
**Recurring service with added incentives.**

Include bonus content, services, or products to increase retention and perceived value.

*Example: Monthly subscription plus quarterly bonus courses or quarterly coaching calls.*

### 3. Discounted Continuity
**Recurring at special rates to encourage long-term commitment.**

Lower ongoing price to lock in customer retention for extended periods.

*Example: First month at full price, then 30% discount for annual commitment.*

### 4. Lifetime Upgrades
**Ongoing value additions throughout customer relationship.**

Provide continuous upgrades or enhancements as part of the continuity model. Customers receive increasing value over time, making it harder to leave.

*Example: Software that gets new features quarterly, membership that adds premium content regularly.*

### 5. Discount + One Time Fee Continuity
**Combined upfront and recurring payment model.**

Initial fee plus ongoing discounted rate creates immediate value perception while locking in long-term relationship.

*Example: Setup fee of $500 plus $99/month for ongoing service with locked-in discount.*

## Key Principle
Good continuity offers make money while acquiring customers through the initial offer, not after. The ideal model gets paid upfront for services that will be delivered over time, removing cash constraints on growth.
