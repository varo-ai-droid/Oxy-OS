# Scale vs Skill

## Definition
The **Scale vs Skill** framework helps determine when to improve your personal capabilities versus when to build systems and hire to scale. It's fundamentally about understanding leverage and creating growth beyond your individual capacity.

## The Core Decision

### Scale When:
- You've maxed out on effectiveness improvements
- Your skills are already above market average
- You can afford to hire (profit per customer is sufficient)
- The work can be standardized and documented

### Improve Skills When:
- You're the bottleneck for critical tasks
- Your skills are below industry standard
- You can't yet afford to hire
- The work requires creativity or unique judgment

## The What-Why-How Framework

When employees aren't performing, it's typically a **manager problem**, not an employee problem. Managers should assume fault and diagnose using:

### What - Do They Know What You Want?
**Problem:** Lack of clear communication about expectations

**Solutions:**
- Write down all expectations in digital form (Slack, email, Asana)
- If it's not written, it never happened
- Create checklists and documented processes
- Follow up verbal instructions with written confirmation

### How - Do They Know How to Do It?
**Problem:** Lack of training, skills, or methodology

**Solutions:**
- Provide direct instruction for missing skills
- Purchase relevant training courses
- Pair with high-performing peers
- Create training materials and documentation

### Why - Why Aren't They Doing It?
**Problem:** Lack of motivation or buy-in

**Solutions:**
- Explain **importance** - how their role impacts the bottom line
- Build **buy-in** - connect them to the company mission and vision
- Create sense of ownership, not just employment
- Address "soft culture stuff" - it scales businesses

## Common Combinations of Missing Elements

| Missing Element | Behavior | Solution |
|-----------------|----------|----------|
| Knows What + Motivated, but lacks How | Pretends to be busy, avoids asking for help | Training with grace, no shame culture |
| Motivated + Has Skills, but lacks What | Works on wrong tasks, paralysis | Clear prioritization, written goals |
| Knows What + Knows How, but lacks Motivation | Checked out, disengaged, job searching | Reconnect to mission, address root causes |

## The "Do It Again" Mindset

Alex Hormozi's approach to skill development is simple: if you want to learn something, do it 100 times. Repetition beats perfection.

### Application:
- Instead of "I'm bad at sales" → make 100 sales calls
- Instead of "I can't create content" → create 100 pieces of content
- Instead of "I don't know marketing" → run 100 paid ad variations

## Practical Scaling Approach

### Stage 1: Skill Building (Under $1M)
- Focus on personal effectiveness
- Do the work yourself to learn
- Document everything as you go

### Stage 2: System Building ($1M+)
- Document processes you've proven work
- Hire and train replacements for yourself
- Focus on improving quality, not volume

### Stage 3: Management (Large Scale)
- Coach managers on the What-Why-How framework
- Focus on culture and alignment
- Measure output through KPIs, not activity

## Key Principles

### Culture Scales, Not Just People
The intangible benefits (culture, mission, buy-in) become your competitive advantage at scale.

### Document Everything
If it's not written down, you can't delegate it. Document processes during the skill-building phase.

### Assume Positive Intent
Employees want to succeed. If they're failing, look inward at your communication, training, and motivation.
