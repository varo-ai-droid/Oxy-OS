# Downsell Offers

## Definition
**Downsell Offers** are alternative monetization strategies designed for prospects who decline your primary offer. Instead of losing them entirely, you present a lower-commitment option that still generates revenue.

## Why Downsells Work
- Not everyone can afford or justify your main offer
- Customers who decline once may still have problems to solve
- Lower price points can convert previously resistant prospects
- Creates a path forward rather than a dead end

## The 3 Types of Downsell Offers

### 1. Payment Plan Downsell
**Spread payments over time to reduce upfront commitment.**

Transform a single large payment into manageable installments. Removes the cash barrier for many prospects.

*Example: Instead of $5,000 upfront, offer 6 payments of $997 with a slight premium.*

### 2. Trial with Penalty Downsell
**Limited access with consequences for not upgrading.**

Offer a reduced experience that still delivers value, but with clear negative consequences if they don't upgrade.

*Example: "Try for 30 days, but if you don't upgrade, you lose your data/progress."*

### 3. Feature Downsell
**Reduced feature set at lower price.**

Remove non-essential features to create a more affordable version of your core offer.

*Example: Basic software without premium integrations or advanced analytics.*

## Key Principle
Downsells should still be profitable and serve the customer's core needs, just with reduced scope or different payment terms.
