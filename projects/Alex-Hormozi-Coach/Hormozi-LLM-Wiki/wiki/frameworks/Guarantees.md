# Guarantees

## Definition
Guarantees are the single greatest way to overcome the number one obstacle in sales: **risk**. They reverse the perceived risk entirely, making people feel stupid saying no.

## Core Impact
Strong guarantees can increase conversion rates **2-4x**. The bigger the guarantee, the higher the net increase in total purchases - even if refund rates increase.

## Guarantee Math
Don't be emotional, just do the math:
- Even if refund percentages double (5% to 10%), a 30% increase in sales still results in 23% more net profit

## Four Types of Guarantees

### 1. Unconditional Guarantees
**Concept:** Strongest, no-questions-asked guarantee - essentially a trial where they pay first.

**Risk:** Highest refund potential, but also highest conversion potential.

**Example Wording (Jason Fladlien):**
> "I'm not asking you to decide yes or no today. I'm asking you to make a fully informed decision... The only way you can make a fully informed decision is on the inside, not the outside... If you ain't happy, I ain't happy for any reason whatsoever. If you want your money back, you can get it."

**Pro Tip:** Name your guarantee something cool and descriptive (e.g., "Club a Baby Seal Guarantee").

**Best For:** Lower-ticket B2C businesses.

### 2. Conditional Guarantees
**Concept:** Include terms and conditions tied to client actions.

**Goal:** Align incentives so clients achieve results and don't need the guarantee.

**Examples:**
- "Double/triple money back" if specific conditions aren't met
- Payment exceeding what they paid if results aren't achieved

**Best For:** Most service-based businesses.

### 3. Anti-Guarantees
**Concept:** Designed for high-cost services where refunds would be painful.

**Strategy:** State what you WON'T do or what ISN'T covered.

**Purpose:** Protect against "shitty customers" who buy only because of guarantees but won't put in the work.

### 4. Partial Guarantees
**Concept:** Limited in scope or timeframe.

**Strategy:** Offer specific protections while maintaining boundaries.

## Framework: What Makes a Guarantee Powerful

**Conditional Statement:** "If you do not get X result in Y period of time, we will Z."

The "Z" portion gives it teeth - it must be meaningful and valuable to the prospect.

## Warnings and Considerations

### Shitty Customers
People who buy *only* because of guarantees may not be willing to put in the work. Mitigate by:
- Tying guarantees to client actions
- Using conditional guarantees for high-touch services

### High-Cost Services
For expensive products/services, employ conditional or anti-guarantees to avoid eating significant refund costs.

### Risk Reversal Psychology
The strongest risk reversal creates the highest conversion. Be prepared to back your guarantee - it's a promise, not a marketing gimmick.
