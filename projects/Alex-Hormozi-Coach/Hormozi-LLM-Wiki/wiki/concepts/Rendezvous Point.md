# Rendezvous Point

## Definition
A **Rendezvous Point** is the intersection where what the customer wants perfectly aligns with what the business wants - creating mutual benefit with no trade-offs. It's the sweet spot where both parties achieve their goals simultaneously.

## Core Principle
The best business outcomes occur when customer success and business profit are not at odds. Find or create these moments of perfect alignment.

## Finding Your Rendezvous Point

### The Question Framework:
Ask yourself: **"When does the customer win exactly when I win?"**

### Common Examples:

#### In Service Businesses:
- **Customer wants:** Results delivered fast
- **Business wants:** High prices, happy customers
- **Rendezvous:** Deliver premium results quickly = higher price + better results

#### In Product Businesses:
- **Customer wants:** Lifetime product guarantees
- **Business wants:** High margins, repeat purchases
- **Rendezvous:** Guarantee builds trust = higher conversion + higher perceived value

#### In Education:
- **Customer wants:** Easy learning, quick results
- **Business wants:** Scalable delivery, low support costs
- **Rendezvous:** Simple, step-by-step systems = happy customers + efficient delivery

## Creating Rendezvous Points

### 1. Align Incentives
Structure pricing and delivery so customer success = business success

### 2. Remove Trade-offs
Eliminate situations where helping the customer hurts the business

### 3. Scale Through Alignment
The bigger the rendezvous point, the easier it is to scale

## Real-World Applications

### Gym Launch Example
- **Customer wants:** Better fitness results without effort
- **Gym wants:** Higher membership prices, lower churn
- **Rendezvous Point:** High-touch personal training with done-for-you programs = customers get results, gym gets high prices and retention

### Acquisition.com Model
- **Customer wants:** Free business education
- **Alex wants:** Authority, trust, future business
- **Rendezvous Point:** Free valuable content = audience learning + Alex building market position

## Key Insights

### No Zero-Sum Thinking
In great businesses, the pie grows for everyone. Look for win-win scenarios.

### Design for Alignment
Build processes where doing good by customers automatically does good by business.

### Measure Both Sides
Track customer success metrics AND business success metrics - they should improve together.
