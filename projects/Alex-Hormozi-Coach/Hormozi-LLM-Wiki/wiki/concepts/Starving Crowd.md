# Starving Crowd

## Definition
A **Starving Crowd** is a market with massive, urgent demand. These are people who desperately need your solution and are actively seeking it now.

## Core Principle
**Starving crowd > Offer strength > Persuasion skills**

Demand is paramount. A starving crowd can make even a mediocre offer or sales pitch successful. Don't try to *create* demand; learn to *channel* existing demand that already exists.

## Four Indicators of a Great Market

### 1. Massive Pain
The audience desperately needs your solution. "The pain is the pitch."

### 2. Purchasing Power
The audience can afford your services at the prices you require.

### 3. Easy to Target
You can easily find and market to your ideal customers through:
- Associations
- Mailing lists
- Social groups
- Communities where they congregate

### 4. Growing
The market itself is expanding, providing a "tailwind" for your efforts.

## Main Markets With Inherent Demand
The three fundamental markets where tremendous pain and demand always exist:
- **Health**
- **Wealth**
- **Relationships**

## Key Insights

### Commit to the Niche
Niches get you riches. Broad markets are competitive and expensive. Specific niches allow for:
- Higher margins
- Lower acquisition costs
- Stronger targeting
- More effective messaging

### Market Selection Process
Choosing the right market is more important than perfecting your offer. A starving crowd in a bad market still beats a great offer in a saturated market.

## Real-World Application

### Finding the Starving Crowd
- Look for people spending money on suboptimal solutions
- Find communities where people complain about lack of good options
- Identify markets where "good enough" solutions are failing
- Research where people are already looking for alternatives

### Testing Market Demand
Before building an elaborate offer, test:
1. Will people pay attention to your message?
2. Will they engage with your content?
3. Will they respond to a simple offer?

If yes to all three, you've likely found a starving crowd.
