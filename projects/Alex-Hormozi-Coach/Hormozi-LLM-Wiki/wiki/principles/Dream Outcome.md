# Dream Outcome

## Definition
The **Dream Outcome** is what the prospect ultimately wants to achieve - their ideal end state. In the Value Equation, this is the numerator that multiplies with perceived likelihood of achievement.

## Core Principle
Customers don't want a membership; they want to lose weight. They don't want a gym; they want to be attractive and healthy. Always sell the solution/destination, not the plane flight.

## Deep Human Desires
People have unchanging, deep desires that transcend time and culture:
- To be beautiful
- To be respected
- To be powerful
- To be loved
- To increase status

Your goal is to channel these existing desires through your offer, not create new ones.

## Status as Value Driver
The dream outcome that most directly increases a prospect's status is generally valued the most. Frame benefits in terms of status gained from others' perspectives.

**Example:** "Your golf buddy's jaws will drop" - focuses on external validation and status increase.

## Key Insights

### Status Framing
When comparing two products satisfying the *same* desire, the Dream Outcome value cancels out. The other three variables (likelihood, time, effort) drive the difference.

### Focus on Outcomes, Not Features
Instead of describing features like "24/7 gym access, personal training sessions," focus on:
- "Lose 20 pounds in 6 weeks"
- "Fit into your favorite jeans again"
- "Feel confident at the beach"

### Universal vs. Personal
Some dream outcomes are universal (health, wealth, relationships). Others are specific to niche audiences. Both can be powerful.
