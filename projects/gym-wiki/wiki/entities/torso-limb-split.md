# Torso Limb Split

## Description
A training split that divides the body into torso (chest, back, shoulders) and limbs (arms, legs) on alternating days. Popularized by Dorian Yates as part of his "Blood and Guts" training philosophy.

## Characteristics
- **Torso day**: Chest, back, shoulders, traps
- **Limb day**: Quadriceps, hamstrings, biceps, triceps, calves
- Typically performed 4 days per week (2 torso, 2 limbs)
- Allows higher volume per session with adequate recovery between sessions

## Historical Context
- Used by Dorian Yates during his 6 Mr. Olympia wins
- Part of the maximalist training approach common in the 1990s
- Different from the full body AB splits common in the silver era
