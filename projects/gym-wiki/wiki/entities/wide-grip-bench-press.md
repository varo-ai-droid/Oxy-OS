# Wide Grip Bench Press

## Definition
Bench press variation performed with hands placed significantly wider than shoulder-width, emphasizing the sternal (lower) portion of the pectoralis major.

## Execution
- Grip width exceeds shoulder-width
- Emphasis on lower chest development
- Requires good shoulder mobility
- Often performed with feet elevated or in powerlifting style

## Physiological Characteristics
- **Muscle Activation**: Higher activation of sternal pectoralis major fibers
- **Range of Motion**: Shortened due to wider grip
- **Leverage**: Different moment arms compared to close-grip bench press
- **Neuromechanical Matching**: Different fiber recruitment patterns than standard grip

## Historical Usage
Featured in Bob Hoffman's York Barbell courses from the 1930s-1950s as part of early bodybuilding programs.

## Programming Notes
- Best used as part of broad exercise selection for complete chest development
- Complementary to close-grip and incline variations
- Part of silver era programs that emphasized heavy loads with basic exercises
