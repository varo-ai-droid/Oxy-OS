# Steve Reeves

## Description
Early Mr. Universe winner (won in 1955) and prominent silver era bodybuilder who popularized high-exercise-variety training programs. Known for his aesthetic physique and the famous Steve Reeves program featuring 3 exercises per muscle group.

## Historical Significance
- Won Mr. America and Mr. Universe titles in the pre-steroid era
- Represented the silver era aesthetic - tall, symmetrical, classical physique
- Published training programs in the early 1950s
- Influenced training approaches emphasizing exercise variety over high volume

## Training Contributions

### The Steve Reeves Program
- **Structure**: Full body with 3 different exercises per muscle group
- **Sets**: Single set per exercise (one set of each)
- **Repetitions**: 8-12 reps range
- **Workout Size**: Approximately 24-26 exercises per session
- **Rest**: 2 minutes between sets, 4 minutes between muscle groups

### Notable Exercises
- Shoulders: Behind the neck press, standing lateral raise, seated front raise
- Chest: Wide grip flat bench press, incline dumbbell press, decline dumbbell fly
- Back: Wide grip bent over barbell row, one-arm dumbbell row, flat barbell pullover
- Arms: Standing barbell curl, incline dumbbell curl, seated concentration curl
- Triceps: Overhead dumbbell extension, incline skull crusher, cable pressdown
- Legs: Barbell squat, barbell box squat, hack squat variation
- Hamstrings/Glutes: Stiff leg deadlift, seated good morning, horizontal back extension
- Calves: Bent knee calf raise, seated calf raise, standing single-leg calf raise

## Physiological Insights
- Emphasized exercise variety to achieve regional muscle fiber development
- Recognized that hypertrophy is muscle fiber specific
- Program designed around neuromechanical matching principles
- Used high repetitions (8-12) which required good cardiovascular fitness

## Significance
- Demonstrated that multiple exercises per muscle group could provide comprehensive fiber recruitment
- Program structure aligns with modern understanding of post-workout fatigue and recovery
- Represented transition toward more systematic training approaches in the silver era
