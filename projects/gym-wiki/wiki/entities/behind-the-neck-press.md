# Behind the Neck Press

## Description
An overhead pressing variation where the bar is lowered behind the neck to shoulder level or below, emphasizing rear deltoid and upper trap activation. Common in silver and golden era bodybuilding.

## Form Cues
- Start with bar on traps or slightly behind neck
- Press upward in arc, finishing overhead
- Lower under control back to starting position
- Requires good shoulder mobility
- Can be performed seated or standing

## Historical Context
- Popularized in silver/golden era bodybuilding
- Often programmed alongside other deltoid-focused exercises
- Part of high-volume shoulder routines of the era
- Mentioned in historical routines predating machine-based training

## Considerations
- Shoulder mobility requirements limit accessibility
- Emphasis on rear delts vs front/side delts varies by individual
- Requires careful load management
- Less common in modern programming due to shoulder health concerns
