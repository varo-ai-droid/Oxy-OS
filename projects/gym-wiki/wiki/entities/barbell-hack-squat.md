# Barbell Hack Squat

## Definition
A rear-foot elevated squat variation where the barbell is positioned behind the legs, invented by George Hackenschmidt in the early 1900s. Resembles a hybrid between a deadlift and squat.

## Execution
- Barbell placed behind the legs/lower back
- Heels elevated (similar to front squat stance)
- Knees slightly bent throughout the movement
- Focus on hip and knee extension

## Physiological Characteristics
- **Position**: More contracted position compared to traditional back squat
- **Fiber Recruitment**: Different fiber recruitment pattern than back squat due to altered leverage
- **Stability**: Less stable than machine variations, requiring greater core engagement

## Historical Context
- Created by George Hackenschmidt (early 1900s)
- Later evolved into hack squat machine variations
- Part of early 20th century training routines for full body development

## Programming Notes
- Can be used in daily training protocols when programmed with moderate loads
- Complements traditional squat by providing different fiber recruitment
- Good exercise for implementing Hackenschmidt's daily training philosophy
