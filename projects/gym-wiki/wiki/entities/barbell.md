# Barbell

## Definition
A free weight consisting of a long bar with weights loaded on each end, used for resistance training exercises.

## Historical Context
- Central to pre-machine era bodybuilding programs
- Used in stiff leg deadlifts, hack squats, pullovers, shrugs, and rows
- Required for many fundamental compound movements
- Part of silver era training infrastructure

## Applications
- [[Stiff Leg Deadlift]] - Hip hinge pattern for hamstrings
- [[Barbell Hack Squat]] - Rear-foot elevated squat variation
- [[Barbell Pullover]] - Chest/back expansion movement
- [[Vertical Shrug]] - Upper trap elevation
- Olympic lifts and powerlifting movements

## Advantages
- Allows progressive overload through added weight
- Engages stabilizer muscles
- Enables stretch-under-load positions
- Versatile across movement patterns

## Considerations
- Requires more stabilization than machines
- Can create bilateral force deficit
- May require more warm-up sets
- Learning curve for technique
