# Blood and Guts Training

## Definition
High-intensity training philosophy popularized by Dorian Yates in the 1990s, characterized by heavy loads, low reps, short rest periods, and taking every set to failure.

## Key Principles
- Heavy loads (often 5RM or lower)
- One set per exercise to complete failure
- Short rest periods between sets
- High-intensity focused rather than volume focused
- "Leave it all in the gym" mentality

## Historical Context
- Developed by Dorian Yates, 6-time Mr. Olympia
- Response to overcrowded gyms requiring shorter workout times
- Incorporated cluster set principles for heavy loading
- Part of modern high-intensity training evolution

## Characteristics
- Torso/limb split structure common
- Emphasis on progressive overload
- Rest periods often minimized
- Intensity techniques: drop sets, forced reps, rest pause

## Significance
- Proved high-intensity approaches can produce elite physiques
- Influenced modern "to failure" training discussions
- Shows effectiveness of heavy load stimulation
- Related to stimulating reps concept (maximizing recruitment per set)
