# Barbell Pullover

## Definition
Silver era exercise performed lying supine on a bench with a barbell, emphasizing chest expansion and back development. Executed with light-moderate loads for high repetitions (10-20 reps).

## Execution
- Lie supine on bench, bar held with shoulder-width grip
- Lower bar behind head while keeping arms straight
- Return to starting position above chest
- Emphasize deep breathing and chest expansion
- Performed explosively with light load

## Physiological Characteristics
- **Stretch Position**: Emphasizes stretch-mediated hypertrophy mechanisms
- **Muscle Targeting**: Primarily chest (sternal head), also engages latissimus dorsi
- **Breathing Pattern**: Used as a breathing exercise in silver era programs
- **Rep Range**: High repetitions (10-20) with light load
- **Position**: More stretched position compared to cable pullover

## Historical Usage
- Featured prominently in 1930s-1950s York Barbell programs
- Bob Hoffman emphasized its role in chest development
- Often paired with squats for "chest expansion" philosophy

## Programming Notes
- Part of silver era bulking routines
- Typically performed for 25 total reps per session (multiple sets)
- Used as breathing exercise between other movements
- Complements pressing movements for balanced chest development
