# Quadriceps

## Definition
A group of four muscles (rectus femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus medialis, vastus intermedius) located on the front of the thigh that extend the knee and flex the hip (rectus femoris).

## Anatomy
- **Rectus Femoris**: Also crosses the hip joint, contributing to hip flexion
- **Vastus Lateralis**: Lateral part of quadriceps, extends knee
- **Vastus Medialis**: Medial part, often emphasized for knee stability
- **Vastus Intermedius**: Under rectus femoris, extends knee

## Muscle Fiber Specificity
- Regional variation in fiber recruitment based on exercise selection
- Different regions have different leverage patterns
- Exercise selection affects which fibers are maximally activated
- Frontal plane work may provide different activation patterns

## Training Considerations
- Leg raises and similar exercises target rectus femoris from hip flexion perspective
- Squat patterns primarily train all four muscles through knee extension
- Early bodybuilders recognized potential for regional development
- Equipment limitations affected training approaches historically

## Primary Exercises
- [[Squat]] - Fundamental lower body compound exercise
- [[Barbell Hack Squat]] - Rear-foot elevated barbell squat variation
- Leg press variations
- Hack squat machine

## Historical Context
- Early programs often lacked dedicated quadriceps isolation exercises
- Equipment limitations meant squat variations were primary method
- Later introduction of leg extensions and other isolation methods
- Steve Reeves program included multiple quad exercises including box squats and hack squats

## Evidence
- Muscle fiber specificity applies to quadriceps as with other muscles
- Different squat variations can emphasize different regions
- Regional hypertrophy demonstrated through neuromechanical matching
- Recovery considerations similar to other large muscle groups
