# Periodization

## Definition
Periodization is the systematic planning of training variables (volume, intensity, frequency) over time to optimize performance and prevent overtraining.

## Scientific Basis
- Prevents accommodation (adaptation plateau)
- Manages fatigue and recovery cycles
- Allows for specific emphasis phases

## Evidence
- Undulating periodization shows slight advantages over linear [[Rhea et al. 2002]]
- Block periodization effective for advanced athletes [[Issurin 2008]]
- Simpler plans often perform as well as complex ones for novices

## Practical Applications
- Linear progression: Start with higher volume/lower intensity, progress toward lower volume/higher intensity
- Undulating: Vary training stress within/between weeks
- Consider: What are you preparing for, and when?
