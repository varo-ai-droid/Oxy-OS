# Warm Up

## Definition
Preparation activities performed before resistance training to potentially reduce injury risk and/or enhance workout performance.

## Physiological Mechanisms

### Temperature Increase
- **Mechanism**: Muscle temperature elevation increases actinomyosin cross bridge formation rate
- **Effect**: Faster shortening velocity, higher firing rates for speed-related adaptations
- **Duration**: Requires ~15 minutes of aerobic exercise to achieve
- **Muscle Specific**: Must target specific muscles being trained
- **Hypertrophy Impact**: No significant effect on mechanical tension stimulus

### Post Activation Potentiation (PAP)
- **Effect**: Immediate (1-5 minute) increase in force production across force-velocity spectrum
- **Mechanism**: Actinomyosin filaments move closer together, increasing contraction efficiency
- **Requirements**: High effort single repetition (not necessarily heavy load)
- **Hypertrophy Impact**: Uncertain - may increase mechanical tension efficiency

### Post Activation Performance Enhancement (PAPE)
- **Duration**: Longer lasting (up to ~15 minutes)
- **Mechanism**: Unknown - not muscle temperature or PAP
- **Potential**: May affect mechanical tension stimulus (requires more research)
- **Timing**: Peaks around 8 minutes post-activation

## Injury Prevention
- **Muscle Temperature**: Increased temperature requires more kinetic energy to reach muscle tearing length
- **PAP/PAPE**: May reduce strain injury risk by increasing force production capacity
- **Evidence**: Most common warm-up methods don't achieve physiological benefits

## Practical Applications

### For Hypertrophy Training
- Warm-ups are primarily for injury prevention, not performance enhancement
- Light aerobic exercise for 15+ minutes targets specific muscles
- Consider long-term benefits vs. time investment
- Non-essential for most training scenarios

### For Athletic Training
- More important for speed-related adaptations
- PAP effect beneficial for explosive movements
- Consider movement-specific warm-ups

### Recommendations
- Empty bar plus high-effort single repetition for PAP effect
- For multi-joint exercises (squat, bench): Use different movement (vertical jump, medicine ball throw)
- For machine exercises: Single high-effort rep with the machine
- Consider sauna for muscle temperature benefits (~20-30 minutes)
