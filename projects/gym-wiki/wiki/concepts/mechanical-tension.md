# Mechanical Tension

## Definition
Mechanical tension is the force exerted on muscle fibers during contraction, particularly when muscles are under load. It is considered one of the primary drivers of muscle hypertrophy.

## Scientific Basis
- High mechanical tension activates mechanosensitive pathways (titin, focal adhesion kinase)
- Triggers mTOR signaling pathway for protein synthesis
- Eccentric contractions may generate higher tension at lower metabolic cost

## Evidence
- Heavy loads (70-90% 1RM) produce high tension and promote hypertrophy [[Schoenfeld 2010]]
- Time under tension protocols show benefits at higher loads, less clear at lower loads
- Stretch-under-load may enhance tension-mediated adaptations [[Pedrosa et al. 2022]]

## Practical Applications
- Use challenging loads to maximize tension
- Emphasize controlled eccentric phases
- Consider compound movements for high systemic tension
