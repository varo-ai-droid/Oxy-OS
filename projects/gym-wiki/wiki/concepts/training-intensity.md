# Training Intensity

## Definition
The load or weight used relative to an individual's maximum capacity, typically expressed as a percentage of one-rep max (% 1RM). Intensity is a key training variable that determines which motor units are recruited and the nature of the adaptation.

## Intensity Zones

### Heavy Loads (70-90% 1RM)
- Produce high mechanical tension
- Recruit high-threshold motor units from the first repetition
- Primary driver of both hypertrophy and strength
- Allow stimulating reps from the very first rep

### Moderate Loads (60-70% 1RM)
- Require accumulation of metabolic fatigue to reach high-threshold motor units
- First several reps serve as "warm-up" before stimulating reps begin
- Effective for hypertrophy but may not maximize strength gains

### Light Loads (<60% 1RM)
- Require very high reps to reach failure and recruit high-threshold units
- Create disproportionately high metabolic fatigue relative to mechanical tension
- Less effective for hypertrophy in trained individuals

## Intensity vs Volume Relationship
- Higher intensity requires lower volume to manage fatigue
- Lower intensity allows higher volume but with diminishing returns
- The recoverable volume window narrows as intensity increases
