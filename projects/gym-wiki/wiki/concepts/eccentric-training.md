# Eccentric Training

## Definition
Eccentric training emphasizes the lowering/negative phase of resistance exercises, where muscles lengthen under load. This phase produces higher mechanical tension with lower energy cost.

## Physiological Basis

### Eccentric Advantage
- Higher force output (~120-150% concentric strength)
- Lower metabolic cost during contraction
- More stretch-mediated hypertrophy potential
- Can produce higher mechanical tension at same effort level

### Tension Considerations
- Stretch-under-load triggers titin and focal adhesion kinase pathways
- Enhanced mTOR signaling compared to concentric-only
- May reduce calcium ion accumulation relative to perceived effort
- Allows more reps at same load due to lower metabolic interference

## Applications

### Heavy Load Eccentrics
- Heavy loads already eccentrically emphasize the negative
- 70-90% 1RM produces high eccentric tension
- May produce hypertrophy with less damaging effects

### Specific Eccentric Methods
- Accentuated eccentric loading (heavy negatives)
- Slow eccentric tempo (3-5 seconds)
- Eccentric overload training
- Flywheel training for accommodating resistance

### Practical Use
- Don't need to emphasize eccentrics artificially
- Heavy loads naturally provide eccentric advantage
- Moderate loads may benefit from controlled eccentrics
- Light loads require eccentric focus to reach tension thresholds
