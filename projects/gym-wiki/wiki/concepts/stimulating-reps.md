# Stimulating Reps

## Definition
Stimulating reps are the final repetitions in a set that provide the hypertrophy stimulus. This concept explains why different repetition ranges (3-5 reps vs 20-25 reps) can produce similar hypertrophy when sets are performed to failure with appropriate loads.

## Physiological Basis

### Motor Unit Recruitment Limitation
- The central nervous system has a maximum tolerable perception of effort limit
- At maximum effort, motor unit recruitment is limited by sensation, not by available motor units
- This creates a voluntary activation deficit - especially in larger muscle groups
- Strength-trained individuals have higher thresholds and thus fewer stimulating reps (~5 for trained, ~10-12 for complete beginners)

### The Effort Ceiling Model
Based on Marcora's psychobiological model:
- Every central motor command copies itself to sensory centers, creating perception of effort
- When this reaches maximum tolerable perception, you stop producing more command
- Stimulating reps are those that occur as you approach this ceiling
- The brain prioritizes motor units with best leverage first (neuromechanical matching)

## Evidence

### Load Comparison Studies
- A study comparing 3-5RM vs 20-25RM with same sets to failure found identical hypertrophy
- Volume load was 2.5-4x higher in the light load group, disproving volume load hypothesis
- Suggests stimulating reps per set may be fewer than previously thought (~4-5)

### Training Experience Impact
- Complete beginners/sedentary: Up to 10-12 stimulating reps per set
- 2+ years training experience: ~5 stimulating reps per set (the "classic number")
- The more strength-trained you are, the higher the recruitment threshold, the fewer stimulating reps

### Individual Variation
- Varies by muscle size (larger muscles = bigger voluntary activation deficits)
- Varies by exercise complexity (multi-joint = bigger deficits)
- Varies by training history (high-force activities before training reduce stimulating reps)

## Practical Applications

### Programming Implications
- Sets to failure are necessary to reach stimulating rep zone
- Higher reps in reserve means fewer stimulating reps, potentially reduced hypertrophy
- For advanced trainees: 5 stimulating reps may require 0-2 reps in reserve

### Exercise Selection Impact
- Large muscle mass exercises (squats, deadlifts, presses): Higher voluntary activation deficit
- Single-joint exercises: Lower deficit, potentially more complete recruitment
- Unilateral variations: Can reduce voluntary activation deficit, improving recruitment

### Load Considerations
- Heavy loads (1-5RM): Stimulating reps from first repetition
- Moderate loads (6-15RM): First 5-8 reps build metabolite fatigue to reach stimulating zone
- Light loads (<30% 1RM): No metabolite occlusion, may not reach stimulating zone without BFR
