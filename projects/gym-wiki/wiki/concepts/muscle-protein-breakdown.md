# Muscle Protein Breakdown

## Definition
The process by which muscle proteins are degraded and broken down into amino acids. MPB is the counter-process to muscle protein synthesis (MPS), and the net balance between MPS and MPB determines whether muscle growth occurs.

## Relationship to Hypertrophy
- **MPS alone does NOT equal growth** — requires MPS > MPB for net protein accretion
- Net protein balance (MPS - MPB) determines actual muscle growth
- In non-stressed individuals, MPB is relatively stable and MPS is the primary variable
- Under stress (sleep restriction, caloric restriction), MPB increases dramatically, potentially overriding MPS

## Factors That Increase MPB
- **Sleep restriction**: Increases glucocorticoid response, raising MPB
- **Caloric restriction**: Reduces MPS and increases MPB
- **High-volume training**: Excessive muscle damage increases repair demands
- **Stress**: Systemic stress hormones increase protein degradation
