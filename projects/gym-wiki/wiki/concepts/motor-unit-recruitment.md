# Motor Unit Recruitment

## Definition
Motor unit recruitment is the process by which the nervous system activates groups of muscle fibers to produce force. It follows the size principle and neuromechanical matching, with units recruited in order of increasing size and decreasing leverage.

## Physiological Mechanisms

### Size Principle
- Motor units are recruited from smallest to largest
- Smaller units have fewer fibers, lower force output
- Larger units have more fibers, higher force output
- Also applies within muscles - fibers with better leverage recruited first

### Neuromechanical Matching
- Recruitment based on mechanical leverage, not anatomical boundaries
- Motor units with best leverage are prioritized
- Within-muscle variation: posterior deltoid before lats in rows
- Regional variation: upper glute before lower glute in hip thrusts

### Voluntary Activation Deficit
- Maximum tolerable effort perception limits recruitment
- Not all motor units with leverage are recruited at failure
- Larger muscle groups have bigger deficits
- Multi-joint exercises exacerbate the deficit
- Single-joint/unilateral exercises reduce the deficit

## Measurement and Evidence

### Interpolated Twitch Method
- Electrical stimulus applied during maximal voluntary contraction
- Measures recruitment completeness
- Typical deficits: 10-15% for quads, 2-3% for elbow flexors
- Proves that not all available fibers are recruited

### Fatigue Impact
- Fatigue mechanisms reduce single fiber tension
- Requires more motor units to produce same force
- Creates feeling of effort without actual weakness
- Post-workout fatigue affects subsequent workout recruitment

### Bilateral Force Deficit
- Single limb exercises recruit ~10-15% more units than two-limb
- Stability demands consume neural resources in bilateral
- Reduced muscle mass = reduced voluntary activation deficit

## Practical Applications

### Exercise Selection for Recruitment
- Unilateral variations improve recruitment completeness
- Single-joint exercises better for small muscles
- Multi-joint for large muscles, but expect incomplete recruitment
- Large muscle groups need strategic variation to cover all regions

### Stimulating Reps Connection
- Stimulating reps are high-recruitment repetitions
- Limited by voluntary activation ceiling
- Explains why 3-5RM and 20-25RM can produce similar hypertrophy
- Training experience reduces available stimulating reps per set
