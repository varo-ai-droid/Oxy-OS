# Daily Training

## Definition
A training approach where exercise is performed every day, often with varying exercises or intensities across sessions. Historically associated with early 20th century physical culture figures like George Hackenschmidt.

## Historical Context
- George Hackenschmidt advocated for daily training in the early 20th century
- Silver era bodybuilders often trained 6 days per week with full body routines
- Daily training was more feasible with lower per-session volumes

## Physiological Considerations
- Daily training requires very low per-session volume to avoid fatigue accumulation
- Different muscle groups or movement patterns can be rotated to allow recovery
- Systemic fatigue (supraspinal CNS) may accumulate even with varied exercises
- Most modern evidence supports 3-4x per week per muscle as optimal
