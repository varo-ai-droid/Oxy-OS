# Diffuse Stimulus

## Definition
A training effect where large multi-joint exercises (squats, deadlifts, rack pulls) provide developmental stimulus to small muscle groups throughout the body that are not directly targeted by specific exercises, creating whole-body muscle mass increases.

## Physiological Basis
When standing under heavy loads:
- Multiple small muscle groups throughout the body are subjected to high tension
- These muscles are not classified as primary movers but still experience loading
- Creates "muscle mass increases which you can't pin down to being in one of your major muscle groups"
- Likely due to indirect loading and stabilization demands

## Key Exercises
- **Squats**: Load transfers through trunk and core stabilizers, affecting small supporting muscles
- **Deadlifts**: Heavy posterior chain loading with indirect stimulus to supporting muscle groups
- **Rack Pulls**: Similar diffuse effect from heavy loading in upper body

## Practical Applications
- Different from targeted training (deliberate muscle selection)
- Provides unique stimulus that single-joint exercises cannot replicate
- May contribute to overall "mass" vs "look" distinctions
- Particularly valuable for individuals seeking total body muscle mass over aesthetic-specific development
