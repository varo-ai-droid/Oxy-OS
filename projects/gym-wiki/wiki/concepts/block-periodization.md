# Block Periodization

## Definition
A training approach where training is divided into distinct blocks, each with a specific focus (e.g., hypertrophy, strength, peaking). Each block builds on the adaptations from the previous block.

## Characteristics
- **Block 1 (Accumulation)**: Higher volume, moderate intensity — builds muscle mass and work capacity
- **Block 2 (Intensification)**: Lower volume, higher intensity — converts mass to strength
- **Block 3 (Peaking)**: Very low volume, very high intensity — maximizes strength for competition

## For Hypertrophy
- Block periodization is effective for advanced athletes (Issurin 2008)
- For hypertrophy-focused training, simpler approaches often work as well
- The key variable for hypertrophy is consistent progressive overload within recoverable volume
- Periodization of volume is of limited value because the recoverable window is narrow (1-3 sets per workout)
