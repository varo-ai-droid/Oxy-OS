# Training Frequency

## Definition
The number of times a muscle group is trained per week. Frequency is a key training variable that interacts with volume, intensity, and recovery capacity to determine hypertrophic outcomes.

## Scientific Basis

### The Weekly Net Stimulus Model
Muscle fibers exist in a constant state of either growth or atrophy. The hypertrophy stimulus period lasts approximately **36 hours** post-workout, after which fibers begin returning to baseline. Higher training frequency keeps fibers in a growth state more consistently.

### Key Evidence: 3x vs 2x vs 1x per Week
The hierarchy of training frequency for hypertrophy:
- **3x per week** > **2x per week** > **1x per week**
- Supported by the Coumbe 2023 meta-analysis (119 hypertrophy studies)
- Higher frequency captures more "first set" benefits from each session

### Physiological Mechanisms
1. **Non-linear dose response**: The first set provides the most stimulus; spreading sets across more days captures more first-set effects
2. **Reduced atrophy time**: Less time spent in the atrophy phase between workouts
3. **Better fatigue management**: Lower per-session volume reduces post-workout fatigue accumulation

## Long-Term Study Evidence (Episode 014)

### Duration-Matched Comparisons
- **3x per week for 8 weeks** consistently shows superior results to **2x per week for 12 weeks**
- Shorter, more frequent programs produce better outcomes than longer, less frequent ones
- The additional weeks in lower-frequency programs do NOT compensate for the reduced per-session stimulus

### Practical Examples
- **3x/week**: Training Monday, Wednesday, Friday allows ~48 hours between sessions
- **2x/week**: Training Monday, Thursday leaves ~72 hours between sessions (more atrophy time)
- **1x/week**: 7 days of atrophy between sessions, relying entirely on one high-volume session

## Frequency Considerations by Context

### Trained vs Untrained
- **Untrained individuals**: Can make gains even with suboptimal frequency due to low baseline
- **Trained individuals**: Higher frequency becomes more important as adaptive reserves diminish

### Volume Distribution
- **3x/week**: 2-3 sets per session (total 6-9 sets/week)
- **2x/week**: 3-4 sets per session (total 6-8 sets/week)
- **1x/week**: 4-6 sets per session (total 4-6 sets/week)

### Recovery Considerations
- Higher frequency requires lower per-session volume to avoid fatigue accumulation
- Per-session volume has a greater impact on post-workout fatigue than weekly total
- Full body 3x/week with 3 sets is generally recoverable for most individuals

## Practical Applications

### Recommended Frequencies
- **Full body 3x/week**: Optimal for most natural lifters
- **Upper/Lower 2x each per week**: Good alternative with slightly less lower body frequency
- **Push/Pull/Legs 2x each per week**: Viable for those who enjoy higher training frequency

### Frequency and Split Decisions
- Full body AB splits are optimal — fibers trained every ~5 days stay in growth territory
- Full body ABC splits don't work — fibers would atrophy between sessions
- Upper/Lower splits require the same exercises in both sessions
