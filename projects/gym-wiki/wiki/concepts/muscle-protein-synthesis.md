# Muscle Protein Synthesis

## Definition
The process by which the body synthesizes new muscle proteins. MPS is one component of muscle growth, with muscle protein breakdown (MPB) determining the net protein balance that drives actual hypertrophy.

## Key Mechanisms

### Relationship to Hypertrophy
- **MPS alone does NOT equal growth** - requires MPS > MPB for net protein accretion
- **Muscle Protein Synthesis (MPS)** = rate of protein building
- **Muscle Protein Breakdown (MPB)** = rate of protein degradation
- Net protein balance (MPS - MPB) determines actual muscle growth

### Four Scenarios Where Elevated MPS Does NOT Cause Growth
1. **Non-exercise muscle injury** (bruising) - MPS repairs damage without adding tissue
2. **Protein feeding alone** - MPS replaces existing proteins, no hypertrophy signal
3. **Aerobic exercise** - Can elevate MPS for 72+ hours without muscle growth
4. **Static stretching/sarcomerogenesis** - Adds sarcomeres in series while causing myofibril atrophy

## Critical Time Windows
- MPS-focused analysis valid only in immediate 12-24 hour post-workout period in non-stressed individuals
- Beyond this window, MPB variations make MPS measurements misleading
- Stress (sleep restriction, caloric restriction) dramatically increases MPB, potentially overriding MPS

## Factors Affecting MPS
- **Heavy loads**: Higher MPS in first 24 hours
- **Moderate loads**: Higher MPS in second 24 hours due to increased muscle damage
- **Long rest periods**: Higher MPS in first 24 hours (more hypertrophy stimulus)
- **Glucocorticoid response**: Sleep/stress restriction increases MPB, decreases net protein balance
