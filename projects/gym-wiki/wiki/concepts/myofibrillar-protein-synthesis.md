# Myofibrillar Protein Synthesis

## Definition
Myofibrillar protein synthesis (MPS) is the process by which muscle fibers synthesize new contractile proteins (actin and myosin) in response to training stimuli. It is distinct from MPS in other compartments and represents the repair component of the hypertrophy response.

## Physiological Distinction

### MPS vs Myofibrillar MPS
- Muscle Protein Synthesis includes all protein synthesis in muscle
- Myofibrillar MPS specifically refers to contractile protein synthesis
- MPS in satellite cells, mitochondria, and other compartments also occurs
- Damage repair requires Myofibrillar MPS, but doesn't necessarily cause growth

### MPS vs Hypertrophy
- Elevated MPS alone does NOT equal muscle growth
- MPS > MPB (Muscle Protein Breakdown) required for net accretion
- Repair scenario: MPS elevated to fix damage but no net growth
- Hypertrophy scenario: MPS > MPB with additional structural additions

## Evidence

### Damage Repair Context
- Myofibrillar MPS primarily responds to damage signals
- Non-exercise muscle injury elevates MPS for repair only
- Aerobic exercise can elevate MPS for 72+ hours without hypertrophy
- Protein feeding elevates MPS for replacement, not growth

### Timing Windows
- MPS-focused analysis valid in immediate 12-24 hour post-workout period
- Beyond this window, MPB variations make MPS measurements misleading
- Stress (sleep restriction, caloric restriction) increases MPB
- MPS elevation during recovery may reflect damage repair, not growth

## Practical Applications

### Stimulus Differentiation
- Myofibrillar MPS: Repair of damaged contractile proteins
- Hypertrophy stimulus: Actual fiber enlargement beyond repair
- Heavy loads: Primarily hypertrophy stimulus
- Eccentric/moderate loads: Higher MPS for repair, less net growth

### Programming Implications
- Recovery from damage ≠ growth stimulus
- MPS measurements alone cannot distinguish repair from hypertrophy
- Need MPS + mechanical tension for true hypertrophy
- Sleep and nutrition support MPS but don't provide stimulus
