# Training Volume

## Definition
Training volume refers to the total amount of work performed in a training session or program. In resistance training, it is typically quantified as sets x reps x load, or more simply as total sets per muscle group per week.

## Scientific Basis
Volume is the primary driver of muscle hypertrophy:
- Minimum effective volume threshold exists (~10 sets/week per muscle group)
- Diminishing returns beyond optimal volume
- Individual variation in optimal volume response

## Evidence
- Meta-analysis shows strong dose-response relationship between volume and hypertrophy [[Schoenfeld et al. 2017]]
- 10+ sets per week optimal for most trainees
- Higher volumes may increase risk of overreaching

## Practical Applications
- Track sets per muscle group per week
- Progress volume before intensity for most trainees
- Consider recovery capacity when programming volume
- Distribute volume across 2+ sessions when possible
