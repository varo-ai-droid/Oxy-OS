# Fatigue

## Definition
Fatigue is the temporary reduction in exercise performance — an objective measurement of strength loss, not a subjective feeling. It is distinct from the sensation of tiredness or discomfort.

## Relationship to Post-Workout Fatigue
- Fatigue during and after training is caused by specific physiological mechanisms
- Post-workout fatigue refers to strength loss persisting after the training session ends
- See [[Fatigue Mechanisms]] for detailed coverage of the four primary fatigue types
- See [[Calcium Ion Accumulation]] for the root cause of post-workout fatigue
- See [[Supraspinal Fatigue]] for CNS-based fatigue mechanisms

## Key Principle
Fatigue is a measurable outcome. When exercise scientists use the term, they refer to an objective reduction in exercise performance, not how you feel.
