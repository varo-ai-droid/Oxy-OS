## Concepts

### Training Principles
- [[Progressive Overload]] - Gradual increase of training stress
- [[Periodization]] - Systematic planning of training variables
- [[Linear Periodization]] - Volume decreases, intensity increases over time
- [[Block Periodization]] - Distinct training blocks with specific focuses
- [[Deload]] - Planned reduction in volume to clear accumulated fatigue

### Physiological Mechanisms
- [[Hypertrophy]] - Muscle fiber size increase
- [[Strength]] - Maximal force production
- [[Mechanical Tension]] - Force on muscle fibers during contraction
- [[Training Volume]] - Total work performed (sets x reps x load)
- [[Training Frequency]] - How often a muscle group is trained per week
- [[Training Intensity]] - Load relative to maximum capacity (% 1RM)
- [[Hypertrophy Stimulus Period]] - ~36 hour growth window post-workout
- [[Supraspinal Fatigue]] - CNS fatigue from perception of effort
- [[Neuromechanical Matching]] - Leverage-based fiber recruitment
- [[Calcium Ion Accumulation]] - Root cause of post-workout fatigue
- [[Fatigue Mechanisms]] - Four primary types of post-workout fatigue
- [[Fatigue]] - Objective reduction in exercise performance
- [[Work Capacity]] - Fatigue resistance for training volume
- [[Recovery]] - Return to baseline after training
- [[Muscle Fiber Specificity]] - Fiber-specific hypertrophy adaptation
- [[Muscle Fiber]] - Single contractile cell within skeletal muscle
- [[Isometric Training]] - Static contractions for muscle growth
- [[MYOPS Elevation Correction]] - Capacity correction for elevated protein synthesis (~10-20%)
- [[Secondary Muscle Damage Effect]] - Inflammatory damage causing secondary strength drop
- [[Voluntary Activation Deficit]] - CNS limitation on motor unit recruitment during effort
- [[Bilateral Force Deficit]] - Reduced activation in bilateral vs unilateral movements
- [[Cluster Sets]] - Intra-set rest training method for fatigue management
- [[Dissociation]] - Secondary muscles in compound movements receive insufficient stimulus
- [[Diffuse Stimulus]] - Whole-body loading effect from large multi-joint exercises
- [[Muscle Protein Synthesis]] - Protein building process (MPS ≠ growth)
- [[Muscle Protein Breakdown]] - Protein degradation process (MPB)
- [[mTOR]] - Kinase enzyme regulating muscle protein synthesis
- [[Rest Periods]] - Recovery time between sets/exercises
- [[Non-Linear Dose Response]] - Diminishing returns of additional sets
- [[Set Structure]] - Organization of sets and reps within a session
- [[Training Density]] - Work performed per unit of time
- [[Stretch Mediated Hypertrophy]] - Sarcomerogenesis from passive tension
- [[Post Activation Potentiation]] - Enhanced performance after maximal contraction
- [[Neural Adaptation]] - Nervous system changes improving strength
- [[One Rep Max]] - Maximum weight for a single repetition
- [[Cardiovascular Fitness]] - Heart/lung capacity affecting training performance
- [[Daily Training]] - Training every day approach
